From Digital to Tabletop

Discover how the HomeSalad video game was transformed into the Realms of the Forgotten Path D&D campaign setting, preserving its core themes while reimagining its world through a fantasy lens.

Bridging Two Worlds

The journey from HomeSalad's digital world to the tabletop experience of Realms of the Forgotten Path represents more than a simple adaptation—it's a thoughtful translation of themes, characters, and environmental messages across different storytelling mediums.

This page serves as your guide to understanding the connections between these two worlds, helping players and Dungeon Masters appreciate how the core essence of HomeSalad has been preserved while creating a unique fantasy experience that stands on its own.

"In adapting HomeSalad to the tabletop, we sought not to merely copy its world, but to ask: how would these same environmental and community themes manifest in a world where magic flows through the land and fantastic creatures roam the wilderness?" — Design Philosophy, Realms of the Forgotten Path

Core Themes Translation

The HomeSalad video game and the Realms of the Forgotten Path D&D campaign share fundamental themes, reimagined through different lenses:

Environmental Restoration

HomeSalad (Digital) Realms of the Forgotten Path (Tabletop)
Players restore polluted environments through sustainable gardening practices and community initiatives. Heroes cleanse magical corruption from the land, restore dormant ley lines, and reawaken nature spirits.
Environmental State System tracks area health from 0-10. Magical Vitality System measures the magical health of regions on the same 0-10 scale.
Visual transformation from barren to lush landscapes. Magical transformation from corrupted to enchanted environments with visible magical effects.

Community Building

HomeSalad (Digital) Realms of the Forgotten Path (Tabletop)
Relationship system with NPCs and community reputation. Community Bond Network with settlements and key NPCs providing mechanical benefits.
Community projects require collective effort. Village rituals and protective wards require party cooperation and community participation.
Knowledge sharing through community workshops. Ancient knowledge recovery and magical tradition preservation.

Corporate Resistance vs. Magical Corruption

In HomeSalad, players confront AgroMax, a corporation exploiting resources and threatening community autonomy. In Realms of the Forgotten Path, this conflict is reimagined as the struggle against the Blightbound Consortium, a cabal of necromancers, corrupt merchants, and power-hungry nobles who harness magical corruption for personal gain.

Both antagonistic forces represent the same core idea: short-term exploitation versus long-term sustainability, with the fantasy setting allowing for more dramatic and magical manifestations of environmental harm.

Gameplay Systems Translation

Core gameplay mechanics from HomeSalad have been reimagined for tabletop play:

Environmental State System → Magical Vitality System

HomeSalad's Environmental State System, which tracks the health of different areas on a scale from 0 (degraded) to 10 (restored), has been adapted into the Magical Vitality System for the D&D campaign.

In the tabletop version, each region's magical vitality affects spell potency, encounter types, and available resources. As players restore an area's magical vitality:

  • Nature-based spells become more powerful
  • Friendly fey creatures return to the area
  • Unique magical plants become available for harvesting
  • Corrupted monsters are gradually replaced by neutral or beneficial creatures

View Digital System | View Tabletop System

Gardening Mechanics → Magical Restoration Rituals

The hands-on gardening mechanics of HomeSalad have been transformed into magical restoration rituals that parties can perform to heal corrupted land.

These rituals incorporate elements familiar to D&D players—component gathering, skill checks, and cooperative actions—while preserving the core satisfaction of nurturing growth and seeing tangible environmental improvement.

For example, the "Seed Planting" action becomes the "Ritual of Renewal," requiring components gathered from purified areas, Nature and Arcana checks, and a full moon to complete.

View Digital Mechanics | View Tabletop Rituals

Relationship System → Community Bond Network

HomeSalad's NPC relationship system has been expanded into the Community Bond Network, which tracks not just individual relationships but the party's standing with entire settlements and factions.

As bonds strengthen, players gain mechanical benefits:

  • Access to rare magical items and knowledge
  • Safe havens that provide enhanced resting benefits
  • Allied NPCs who may provide assistance during critical moments
  • Influence over regional politics and decision-making

View Digital System | View Tabletop System

Seasonal Cycles → Magical Convergences

The seasonal gameplay of HomeSalad has been reimagined as magical convergences—periods when certain types of magic are strengthened or weakened based on celestial alignments.

These convergences affect:

  • The effectiveness of certain restoration rituals
  • The types of creatures that might appear
  • The potency of specific magical traditions
  • The accessibility of certain planar connections

This system preserves the cyclical nature of HomeSalad's gameplay while adding a distinctly magical element appropriate for a fantasy setting.
