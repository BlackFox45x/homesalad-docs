Image Prompts

Detailed prompts for illustrations throughout the Realms of the Forgotten Path campaign book.

About These Image Prompts

The illustrations for the Homesalid: Realms of the Forgotten Path campaign book are designed to convey the central themes of environmental restoration, community bonds, and the struggle between corruption and renewal. Each image prompt provides detailed guidance for creating illustrations that effectively communicate these themes while maintaining a cohesive visual style throughout the book.

These prompts are organized by section and include specifications for:

  • Style and artistic approach
  • Composition and focal elements
  • Subject matter and character representation
  • Lighting, color palette, and mood
  • Thematic connections to the campaign's core concepts

Cover Art

Main Cover: "Guardians of Verdantis"

Style: Dramatic fantasy illustration with rich detail and a painterly quality.

Composition: A heroic group of diverse adventurers stands on a ridge overlooking a landscape that transitions from corruption to restoration.

Subject Matter:

  • Foreground: Withered trees, gray soil, and subtle signs of magical corruption
  • Middle ground: A diverse party of adventurers representing the four environmental character concepts
  • Background: Vibrant forest with healthy trees, clear streams, and subtle magical elements

Lighting/Mood: Dawn lighting with golden rays breaking through clouds, symbolizing hope and renewal.

Thematic Connection: The image visually represents the campaign's core theme of environmental restoration.

Character Sheets Section

Environmental State Tracker Illustration

Style: Clean, instructional illustration with a decorative fantasy border.

Composition: A circular or semi-circular gauge showing the Environmental State scale from 0-10.

Subject Matter:

  • Level 0-2 (Critical): Barren, twisted landscape with visible corruption
  • Level 3-4 (Degraded): Struggling environment with some dead vegetation
  • Level 5-6 (Stressed): Recovering landscape with mixed healthy and unhealthy elements
  • Level 7-8 (Healthy): Vibrant, thriving ecosystem
  • Level 9-10 (Pristine): Magically enhanced landscape with visible ley line energy

Lighting/Mood: Neutral, clear lighting that allows for easy reading of the gauge.

Community Bond Network Map

Style: Decorative cartographic style with fantasy elements.

Composition: A network diagram showing interconnected settlements with paths between them.

Subject Matter:

  • Central settlements of Verdantis represented by small illustrated icons
  • Connecting paths with decorative elements representing different types of relationships
  • Empty nodes where players can add new communities they discover

Lighting/Mood: Warm, inviting colors that evoke a sense of community and connection.

Monsters Section

Corrupted Creatures Showcase

Style: Detailed, slightly dark fantasy illustration with a scientific/naturalist approach.

Composition: A collection of corrupted creatures arranged in a dynamic composition.

Subject Matter:

  • Blightwood Shambler: A once-majestic treant now twisted by corruption
  • Corruption Elemental: An amorphous entity composed of swirling pollution
  • Blightfang Predator: A wolf or big cat with mutations like extra eyes or crystalline growths
  • Tainted Dryad: A once-beautiful forest spirit now spreading corruption

Lighting/Mood: Eerie, unnatural lighting with sickly purple and green highlights.

Environmental Guardians

Style: Majestic, luminous fantasy illustration with an emphasis on the connection between creatures and their environments.

Composition: A collection of guardian creatures in their natural environments.

Subject Matter:

  • Verdant Sentinel: A humanoid figure composed of living plants, wood, and glowing magical energy
  • Ley Line Warden: A translucent, ethereal being with flowing energy patterns
  • Ancient Treant: A massive, ancient tree-being with a face formed from bark and moss
  • Restoration Sprites: Tiny, luminous fey creatures that heal small areas of corruption

Lighting/Mood: Warm, golden light with magical luminescence.

Magic Items Section

Restoration Items Collection

Style: Detailed magical item illustration with a focus on craftsmanship and magical properties.

Composition: A collection of restoration-focused magic items arranged on a naturalist's workbench.

Subject Matter:

  • Seedling of Renewal: A small, ever-blooming seedling in a crystal vial
  • Purifier's Staff: A wooden staff with crystal formations growing naturally along its length
  • Verdant Heart Amulet: An amulet containing a fragment of the Great Ley Line Confluence
  • Cloak of Environmental Adaptation: A cloak that shifts its appearance to match different environments

Lighting/Mood: Soft, magical lighting with each item emitting its own subtle glow.

The Verdant Heart Artifact

Style: Dramatic, detailed magical artifact illustration with an emphasis on otherworldly power.

Composition: A central focus on the artifact itself, with its effects radiating outward.

Subject Matter:

  • The Verdant Heart: A crystalline orb containing a miniature ecosystem
  • Surrounding the orb, waves of restorative energy transforming corrupted land
  • Small nature spirits drawn to the artifact's power

Lighting/Mood: Radiant, powerful light emanating from the artifact itself.

Adventure Modules Section

Chapter Illustrations Overview

Each chapter requires a key illustration that captures its central theme:

  • Chapter 1: "Seeds of Renewal" - The party discovering an ancient seed vault hidden beneath a corrupted forest
  • Chapter 2: "Roots of Resistance" - The heroes rallying communities to resist environmental exploitation
  • Chapter 3: "Branches of Knowledge" - The heroes uncovering lost environmental knowledge in an ancient library
  • Chapter 4: "Leaves of Change" - A large-scale restoration ritual being performed by the heroes and their allies
  • Chapter 5: "Heart of Verdantis" - The final confrontation at the Great Ley Line Confluence

Each illustration should maintain a consistent style while highlighting the progression of the campaign's environmental themes from discovery to resistance to knowledge to restoration to final confrontation.
