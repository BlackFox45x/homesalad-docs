The Withering Spring

A starter adventure for 1st-level characters focused on environmental restoration in Verdantis

Adventure Overview

Levels: 1-3

Party Size: 3-6 players

Setting: The village of Greenhollow and the surrounding Verdant Heart

Duration: 3-4 sessions (8-12 hours of gameplay)

Environmental State: Declining from 7 to 4 during the adventure

In this introductory adventure, the characters arrive in the thriving village of Greenhollow, nestled within the Verdant Heart region. The village is known for its harmonious relationship with nature and the magical spring at its center that nourishes the surrounding forest. However, something is wrong—the spring's waters are gradually turning murky, plants near it are withering, and animals are falling ill.

As the characters investigate, they discover that a mining operation run by the Arcane Exploitation League has begun tapping into a ley line that feeds the spring. Their reckless extraction methods are causing magical corruption to seep into the groundwater, threatening not just Greenhollow but the entire region's environmental health.

The adventure serves as an introduction to the Environmental State System and Community Bond Network, as players work to identify the source of corruption, rally the community, and restore balance to the spring before the damage becomes irreversible.

"Our spring has nourished this forest for generations. Its waters carry not just life, but magic—the very essence of the Verdant Heart. If it falls, so too will the balance we've worked so hard to maintain." — Elder Thorne, Greenhollow's druid caretaker

For Dungeon Masters

This adventure is designed to introduce both players and Dungeon Masters to the environmental themes of the Verdantis campaign setting. It showcases several key elements:

  • Environmental State System - Players will witness and track the declining health of the region as corruption spreads
  • Community Bond Network - Characters will form relationships with Greenhollow residents and work to rally them against the threat
  • Magical Corruption - The adventure demonstrates how environmental damage manifests magically in this setting
  • Restoration Activities - Players will learn various methods for healing corrupted environments
  • Opposing Factions - The conflict between the Verdant Covenant and the Arcane Exploitation League is introduced

Running This Adventure

This adventure is structured to allow for significant player agency while maintaining a coherent narrative. The events and encounters can be rearranged or modified to suit your group's playstyle and interests.

Pay special attention to the "Forgotten Memories" sections throughout the adventure. These provide opportunities for characters to experience flashbacks that reveal important information about the setting and the current situation.

For new Dungeon Masters, suggested dialogue and detailed encounter information is provided. Experienced DMs may wish to adapt these elements to suit their own style.

Adventure Background

The Verdant Heart has long been one of the healthiest regions in Verdantis, with an Environmental State of 7-8 in most areas. The village of Greenhollow sits at the center of a network of ley lines that converge at the Verdant Spring, a magical wellspring that nourishes the surrounding forest and maintains the region's ecological balance.

Three months ago, the Arcane Exploitation League established a mining operation in the nearby foothills, ostensibly to extract rare minerals for magical research. In reality, they're tapping directly into one of the ley lines that feeds the Verdant Spring, extracting raw magical energy using experimental and reckless methods.

This extraction has begun corrupting the ley line, causing magical pollution to seep into the groundwater and contaminate the Verdant Spring. The corruption is subtle at first—slightly discolored water, withering plants, and minor animal illnesses—but is accelerating rapidly. If not addressed, it will cause irreversible damage to the entire ecosystem.

The Verdant Covenant

This alliance of druids, rangers, and nature-aligned communities has protected the Verdant Heart for generations. They've noticed the early signs of corruption but haven't yet identified the source. Their representative, Willow Greenmantle, has arrived in Greenhollow to investigate and will become an important ally to the players.

The Arcane Exploitation League

This corporate entity specializes in extracting magical resources for profit, with little regard for environmental consequences. Their local operation is led by Overseer Vex Ironhand, who is under pressure from his superiors to increase production. The League employs both miners and mercenaries to protect their interests.

Greenhollow Village

The village has existed in harmony with the forest for centuries, with traditions and practices that maintain environmental balance. Elder Thorne, the village's druid caretaker, has been performing rituals to slow the spring's corruption, but his powers are waning as the contamination grows. The villagers are divided between those who recognize the threat and those who have been bribed or deceived by the League.

Adventure Hooks

Here are several ways to bring the characters to Greenhollow and involve them in the adventure:

Environmental Guardians

The characters are members or allies of the Verdant Covenant who have been sent to investigate reports of environmental degradation in the Verdant Heart region. Willow Greenmantle provides them with a map to Greenhollow and asks them to determine the cause and severity of the problem.

Mysterious Illness

A friend or relative of one of the characters lives in Greenhollow and has fallen ill with a strange malady that local healers can't cure. Upon investigation, the characters discover the illness is connected to the corrupted spring water.

Magical Disturbance

Spellcasting characters have noticed their magic becoming unstable or weakened when traveling through the region. They've traced the disturbance to the Verdant Heart area and arrive in Greenhollow seeking answers.

Job Opportunity

The characters see a job posting from the Arcane Exploitation League seeking guards or specialists for their mining operation. This gives them an opportunity to infiltrate the organization and discover their true activities from the inside.

Environmental Connection

This adventure works best if at least some characters have backgrounds or classes connected to environmental themes. Druids, rangers, and characters with the Nature Warden or Reclaimer backgrounds will have particularly meaningful roles to play. However, all character types can contribute to the restoration efforts in different ways.

Part 1: Trouble in Greenhollow

The adventure begins as the characters arrive in the village of Greenhollow, located in the heart of the Verdant Heart region. The village is built in harmony with the surrounding forest, with buildings that incorporate living trees and plants into their structures.

Greenhollow Village

As the characters approach, read or paraphrase the following:

The forest path opens into a sunlit clearing where the village of Greenhollow spreads before you. Unlike conventional settlements, Greenhollow seems to grow from the forest itself. Homes are built around living trees, with walls of woven branches and roofs of carefully cultivated moss and flowering vines. Bridges of twisted roots connect tree-dwellings, while ground structures blend seamlessly with the surrounding vegetation.

At the village center stands an ancient oak tree of immense proportions, its canopy spreading protectively over the settlement. At its base, a clear spring bubbles up from the earth, its waters flowing through a series of channels that irrigate gardens and provide water to the village.

As you draw closer, however, you notice concerning details. Some of the spring's channels contain water with a slight grayish tinge. Patches of plants near these channels appear wilted or discolored. Villagers gather in small groups, speaking in hushed, worried tones while occasionally glancing toward the great oak and its spring.

Key Locations in Greenhollow

  • The Verdant Spring - The magical spring at the base of the great oak, now showing signs of contamination
  • The Leafy Bough Inn - A welcoming establishment built around three intertwined trees, run by Mira Oakenheart
  • Elder's Grove - Where Elder Thorne conducts rituals and village meetings
  • Herbalist's Cottage - Home and shop of Sage Brenna, who's been treating animals affected by the contamination
  • Community Gardens - Shared agricultural spaces showing patchy signs of blight

Initial Encounters

During their first day in Greenhollow, the characters can interact with various villagers and gather information about the environmental problems. Key NPCs include:

Elder Thorne (Village Druid)

Appearance: A weathered half-elf with bark-like skin and hair that changes color with the seasons (currently showing hints of autumn despite it being spring).

Personality: Wise and patient, but increasingly worried about the spring's corruption.

Information: Elder Thorne explains that the Verdant Spring is connected to a network of ley lines that maintain the region's magical balance. He's been performing purification rituals, but the corruption is spreading faster than he can cleanse it. He suspects an external source but lacks the strength to venture far from the spring.

Quest: Elder Thorne asks the characters to collect water samples from upstream sources to help identify the contamination's origin. He provides special containers enchanted to preserve the samples' magical properties.

Willow Greenmantle (Verdant Covenant Agent)

Appearance: A young human woman with green-tinged hair and eyes, wearing practical leather armor adorned with living vines.

Personality: Passionate and direct, with a fierce commitment to environmental protection.

Information: Willow arrived in Greenhollow three days ago after Verdant Covenant monitoring detected a decline in the region's Environmental State. She's been mapping affected areas and has noticed a pattern pointing toward the foothills where the mining operation is located.

Quest: Willow asks the characters to help her investigate the mining site, as she suspects corporate sabotage but needs evidence before the Covenant can take action.

Sage Brenna (Village Herbalist)

Appearance: A middle-aged human with callused hands and clothes stained with plant dyes and medicinal preparations.

Personality: Practical and knowledgeable, with a dry sense of humor that masks her concern.

Information: Brenna has been treating animals affected by drinking from contaminated streams. She's collected samples of affected plants and can show the characters how the contamination manifests physically—plants with unnatural growth patterns, animals with minor mutations, and water that resists certain purification methods.

Quest: Brenna needs specific herbs from the forest to create remedies, but the usual gathering spots are in areas now showing signs of corruption. She asks the characters to gather ingredients while taking precautions against exposure.

Dorn Ironfoot (Mining Company Representative)

Appearance: A well-dressed dwarf with an expensive cloak and multiple rings, who seems out of place in the rustic village.

Personality: Smooth-talking and evasive, with practiced responses to environmental concerns.

Information: Dorn claims to represent the "Verdant Mining Collective" (a false name for the Arcane Exploitation League) and insists their operation follows all proper procedures. He's in the village offering compensation to anyone experiencing "temporary inconveniences" from the mining work.

Encounter: The characters might observe Dorn bribing village council members or intimidating those who speak out against the mining operation.

Environmental Assessment

During their exploration of Greenhollow, the characters can observe the effects of the declining Environmental State:

As you move through the village and surrounding forest, you notice subtle but concerning changes in the environment. Near the spring's origin, the water remains relatively clear, but as it flows through the channels, a grayish tinge becomes more pronounced. Plants growing alongside these channels show signs of stress—leaves curling at the edges, flowers failing to bloom fully, and some younger plants withering entirely.

Small animals behave unusually. Squirrels and birds move with less coordination, some appearing disoriented. A rabbit near one of the contaminated streams has patchy fur and slightly enlarged eyes that glow faintly in shadow. Fish in the clearer parts of the stream avoid the contaminated water flowing in from tributaries.

Most telling is the great oak at the village center. While still magnificent, close inspection reveals sections of bark developing an ashen color, and some of its leaves have an unnatural bluish tint at their edges. The magical aura that once radiated visibly from the tree has dimmed, flickering like a candle in the wind.

Environmental State Mechanics

This section of the adventure demonstrates the Environmental State System in action. The Verdant Heart region normally maintains an Environmental State of 7-8, but the area around Greenhollow has declined to 5 and continues to drop as the corruption spreads.

Characters with proficiency in Nature, Survival, or Arcana can make checks (DC 12) to assess the Environmental State more precisely. Druids, rangers, and characters with the Nature Warden or Reclaimer backgrounds have advantage on these checks.

As the adventure progresses, the Environmental State will continue to decline unless the characters take action, with increasingly severe consequences for the ecosystem and the village.

Adventure Outline

This starter adventure continues with the following sections:

  • Part 2: Tracking the Source - Characters follow the contamination upstream to discover the mining operation
  • Part 3: The Mining Operation - Infiltration of the Arcane Exploitation League's facility and discovery of their ley line extraction methods
  • Part 4: Restoration Efforts - Characters work with the village to cleanse the spring and begin healing the environment
  • Part 5: Confrontation - Final confrontation with Overseer Vex Ironhand and the League's mercenaries
  • Epilogue - Long-term restoration plans and consequences of the characters' actions

Complete adventure details will be added in a future update.

Environmental Restoration

Throughout this adventure, characters will learn and apply various restoration techniques:

  • Physical Cleanup: Removing contaminated soil, redirecting water flow, and planting purifying vegetation
  • Magical Purification: Casting restoration spells, performing cleansing rituals, and creating magical filters
  • Community Organization: Rallying villagers to assist in restoration efforts, teaching sustainable practices, and building community bonds
  • Ley Line Repair: Healing the damaged ley line and redirecting its flow away from the mining operation

These activities directly improve the Environmental State and establish Community Bonds that will benefit the characters throughout the campaign.

Adventure Continuation

This starter adventure serves as an introduction to the environmental themes of the Verdantis campaign and leads directly into Chapter 1: Seeds of Renewal. Characters who complete this adventure should advance to 3rd level and gain their first connections to the Verdant Covenant and other environmental factions.

The mining operation is just one of many environmental threats facing Verdantis, and the characters' actions here will establish their reputation as guardians of the natural world.
