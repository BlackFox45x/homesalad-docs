Character Creation

Create heroes dedicated to restoring balance to the natural world of Verdantis.

Creating Characters for Verdantis

The heroes of Verdantis are more than just adventurers seeking glory and treasure—they are guardians of a wounded world, champions of balance, and catalysts for positive change. Creating a character for this campaign means crafting a hero whose personal journey is intertwined with the fate of the natural world and the communities that depend on it.

"The path of restoration requires many different talents. Some heal the land with magic, others defend it with steel, and still others inspire communities to change. What matters is not how you contribute, but that you do." — Willow Greenmantle, Keeper of the Verdant Covenant

This guide will walk you through creating a character uniquely suited to the environmental and community themes of the Homesalid: Realms of the Forgotten Path campaign. While you can use any official D&D 5e character options, this guide focuses on the custom options designed specifically for this setting.

Connection to HomeSalad

The character concepts and roles in this campaign are fantasy reflections of the player's journey in the HomeSalad video game. Just as the HomeSalad player progresses from newcomer to community leader while restoring the environment, your D&D character will follow a similar heroic path in a magical context.

For more information on how character progression works in the original game, see the Character Progression documentation.

Character Creation Steps

Follow these steps to create a character for the Verdantis campaign:

  1. Choose a Concept - Decide what kind of environmental guardian you want to play
  2. Select a Race - Choose from standard D&D races or Verdantis-specific variants
  3. Choose a Class - Select a class and subclass that fits your concept
  4. Determine Ability Scores - Assign your character's core attributes
  5. Select a Background - Choose a background that connects you to Verdantis
  6. Define Your Environmental Connection - Establish your character's relationship with nature
  7. Establish Community Bonds - Determine your character's ties to communities
  8. Choose Equipment - Select starting gear with an emphasis on sustainability
  9. Add Final Details - Complete your character with personality traits, ideals, bonds, and flaws

Environmental Character Concepts

Before choosing specific mechanics, consider what type of environmental guardian you want to play:

The Restorer

You directly heal corrupted environments using magic, rituals, or specialized knowledge. Your focus is on reversing damage and bringing life back to wounded places.

Suggested Classes: Druid (Circle of Restoration), Cleric (Nature Domain), Ranger (Guardian Conclave), Wizard (School of Geomancy)

The Defender

You protect vulnerable environments and the creatures that inhabit them from those who would exploit or corrupt them. You stand as a shield between nature and its enemies.

Suggested Classes: Ranger (Guardian Conclave), Fighter (Green Knight), Paladin (Oath of the Ancient), Barbarian (Path of the Verdant Guardian)

The Community Builder

You focus on helping communities develop sustainable relationships with their environments. You're a diplomat, teacher, and inspiration for positive change.

Suggested Classes: Bard (College of Community), Cleric (Community Domain), Rogue (Sustainability Scout), Artificer (Eco-Engineer)

The Knowledge Keeper

You preserve and recover ancient environmental wisdom, researching sustainable practices from the past to apply to present challenges.

Suggested Classes: Wizard (School of Geomancy), Monk (Way of the Four Elements), Artificer (Eco-Engineer), Druid (Circle of Restoration)

Environmental Connection

A unique aspect of character creation for this campaign is defining your character's relationship with the natural world. This connection influences how you interact with the Environmental State System and what special abilities you might develop as you restore corrupted regions.

Environmental Affinities

Choose one or two environmental affinities that represent the types of ecosystems your character is most connected to:

  • Forest Affinity: Connection to woodlands, groves, and forest ecosystems
  • Water Affinity: Connection to rivers, lakes, oceans, and aquatic life
  • Mountain Affinity: Connection to highlands, caves, and rocky terrain
  • Plains Affinity: Connection to grasslands, farmlands, and open spaces
  • Desert Affinity: Connection to arid landscapes and their unique adaptations
  • Urban Affinity: Connection to the intersection of civilization and nature

Your affinity grants advantage on Environmental State checks in corresponding regions and may unlock special restoration abilities for those environments.

Environmental Motivation

Define why your character is committed to environmental restoration:

  • Ancestral Duty: Your family has always been stewards of the land
  • Personal Loss: You've witnessed the destruction of a place you loved
  • Spiritual Calling: Nature spirits or deities have chosen you
  • Scientific Interest: You study ecological systems and want to preserve them
  • Community Welfare: You understand that healthy environments support healthy communities
  • Redemption: You once contributed to environmental harm and seek to make amends

Your motivation provides roleplaying hooks and may influence how NPCs and factions respond to you.

Connection to HomeSalad

The Environmental Connection system is inspired by the Gardening Specializations in HomeSalad, where players can focus on different types of plants and ecosystems. It also reflects the Motivation System that drives the player's actions in the video game.

Community Bonds

In addition to your connection with nature, your character has relationships with the communities of Verdantis. These bonds provide both narrative hooks and mechanical benefits as you progress through the campaign.

Starting Community Connections

Choose one of the following starting community connections:

Local Hero

You've already made a positive impact in a small community. Choose one settlement where you begin with a Friendly (3) Community Bond level.

Benefit: You have a safe haven in this community and can call on its residents for basic assistance.

Faction Affiliate

You have ties to one of the major factions of Verdantis. Choose one faction where you begin with a Friendly (3) Community Bond level.

Benefit: You have access to faction resources and can request specialized assistance related to the faction's expertise.

Wandering Restorer

You've traveled widely, helping many communities in small ways. You begin with a Neutral (2) Community Bond level with three different settlements of your choice.

Benefit: You have a network of contacts across Verdantis and basic knowledge of multiple regions.

Outsider

You're new to Verdantis or have lived in isolation. You have no established Community Bonds, but you gain advantage on your first three Community Bond checks as people are intrigued by your fresh perspective.

Benefit: You're unbiased by existing relationships and can more easily forge new paths.

Connection to HomeSalad

The Community Bonds system is based on the Relationship System in HomeSalad, where players build connections with NPCs and community groups. It translates the social aspect of the video game into the collaborative storytelling format of D&D.
