Monsters of Homesalid

Discover the unique creatures that inhabit the Realms of the Forgotten Path.

Bestiary Introduction

The world of Homesalid is home to a diverse array of creatures, from familiar beasts to unique entities found nowhere else in the multiverse. This bestiary catalogs the monsters that adventurers might encounter during their journeys through the Realms of the Forgotten Path.

Each entry provides game statistics, lore, and tactical information to help Dungeon Masters incorporate these creatures into their campaigns. The monsters are organized by type and challenge rating for easy reference.

"To understand a land, study its predators. To understand its people, study what they fear." — Ranger Captain Thorne Wayfinder

Using This Bestiary

The creatures in this bestiary are designed specifically for the Homesalid setting, but many can be adapted for use in other campaign worlds. Each entry includes notes on how the creature fits into the ecology and lore of Homesalid, as well as suggestions for encounters and adventure hooks.

Challenge ratings (CR) are provided as a guideline, but remember that terrain, tactics, and context can significantly affect the difficulty of an encounter. Always adjust encounters to suit your party's capabilities and the story you want to tell.

Native Creatures

These creatures evolved naturally in the world of Homesalid, adapting to its unique environments and magical conditions.

Mistwalker

Medium monstrosity, neutral

Armor Class: 14 (natural armor)

Hit Points: 45 (6d8 + 18)

Speed: 50 ft.

STR 14 (+2)
DEX 18 (+4)
CON 16 (+3)
INT 7 (-2)
WIS 14 (+2)
CHA 10 (+0)

Skills: Perception +4, Stealth +6

Damage Resistances: Cold

Senses: Darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 14

Languages: —

Challenge: 2 (450 XP)

Mist Camouflage. The mistwalker has advantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks made to hide in misty or foggy conditions.

Keen Senses. The mistwalker has advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on hearing or smell.

Mist Step. Once per day, the mistwalker can cast misty step as a bonus action, without requiring components.

Actions

Multiattack. The mistwalker makes two attacks: one with its antlers and one with its hooves.

Antlers. Melee Weapon Attack: +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 7 (1d8 + 3) piercing damage.

Hooves. Melee Weapon Attack: +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 6 (1d6 + 3) bludgeoning damage.

Mist Cloud (Recharge 5-6). The mistwalker exhales a cloud of supernatural mist in a 15-foot cone. Each creature in that area must make a DC 13 Constitution saving throw. On a failed save, a creature takes 10 (3d6) cold damage and is blinded until the end of its next turn. On a successful save, the creature takes half as much damage and isn't blinded.

Mistwalker Lore

Mistwalkers are deer-like creatures native to the Shadowfen region of Homesalid. Their translucent skin reveals a ghostly outline of their skeletal structure and internal organs, giving them an eerie appearance, especially in fog or mist where they are often mistaken for spirits.

These creatures have adapted to life in the perpetually misty swamps by developing the ability to manipulate mist and fog. They use this ability both defensively to escape predators and offensively when hunting or protecting their territory.

Mistwalkers travel in small herds of 3-5 individuals and are most active at dawn and dusk. They feed primarily on a type of luminescent fungi that grows in the Shadowfen, which is believed to be the source of their supernatural abilities.

The antlers of a mistwalker are highly valued by alchemists and spellcasters for their magical properties, particularly in potions and items related to concealment and illusion.

Encounter Ideas

  • A herd of mistwalkers has been driven from their territory by a more dangerous predator and now threatens travelers on a major road.
  • A local alchemist offers a reward for intact mistwalker antlers, but warns that they must be harvested during the full moon to retain their magical properties.
  • Villagers report seeing "ghost deer" near an ancient burial ground, leading to superstitious fears that the dead are rising.

Additional native creatures include:

  • Thornback Ravager (CR 3) - Massive boar-like creatures with crystalline growths along their spines
  • Luminous Stalker (CR 4) - Predatory felines that can absorb and emit light
  • Deepmire Serpent (CR 5) - Enormous swamp snakes with paralytic venom
  • Crystal Burrower (CR 1) - Rodents that consume minerals and develop crystalline armor
  • Duskwing (CR 1/2) - Bat-like creatures that can temporarily drain color from their surroundings

Veil Entities

These mysterious beings exist partially in the Veil, the enigmatic plane between realities that is unique to the Homesalid setting. Veil entities often have abilities related to memory, time, or dimensional manipulation.

Memory Leech

Small aberration, chaotic neutral

Armor Class: 13

Hit Points: 22 (5d6 + 5)

Speed: 0 ft., fly 30 ft. (hover)

STR 6 (-2)
DEX 16 (+3)
CON 12 (+1)
INT 14 (+2)
WIS 13 (+1)
CHA 11 (+0)

Skills: Perception +3, Stealth +5

Damage Immunities: Psychic

Condition Immunities: Charmed, Frightened

Senses: Darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 13

Languages: Understands Common but can't speak, telepathy 60 ft.

Challenge: 3 (700 XP)

Memory Sense. The memory leech can detect the presence of intelligent creatures within 60 feet by sensing their memories. This sense works even against invisible creatures or those hidden by illusion magic.

Incorporeal Movement. The memory leech can move through other creatures and objects as if they were difficult terrain. It takes 5 (1d10) force damage if it ends its turn inside an object.

Veil Shift. As a bonus action, the memory leech can partially shift into the Veil, becoming translucent and gaining resistance to nonmagical attacks until the start of its next turn.

Actions

Memory Drain. Melee Spell Attack: +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one creature. Hit: 10 (3d6) psychic damage, and the target must succeed on a DC 13 Intelligence saving throw or lose one memory. The memory leech chooses which type of memory to target: recent (events from the past day), significant (important personal events), or skill-related (proficiency with a specific skill or tool). The effects depend on the type of memory drained:

  • Recent: The target has disadvantage on Intelligence checks related to events from the past day.
  • Significant: The target has disadvantage on Wisdom saving throws for 1 hour.
  • Skill-related: The target loses proficiency with one skill or tool for 1 hour.

Memory Projection (Recharge 5-6). The memory leech projects a recently consumed memory in a 15-foot cone. Each creature in that area must make a DC 13 Wisdom saving throw. On a failed save, a creature becomes stunned for 1 round as it is overwhelmed by the foreign memory. Additionally, any creature that fails the save by 5 or more also takes 7 (2d6) psychic damage.

Memory Leech Lore

Memory leeches are entities that exist partially in the Veil, the mysterious plane that contains forgotten memories and lost knowledge. They appear as translucent, jellyfish-like creatures with pulsing, luminescent bodies and numerous tendrils that glow with an eerie blue light.

These creatures feed on memories, particularly emotionally charged or significant ones. They are drawn to areas where the boundary between the Material Plane and the Veil is thin, such as ancient ruins, sites of great tragedy or joy, or places where powerful magic has been worked.

Memory leeches are not inherently malevolent—they feed on memories much as natural predators feed on prey. However, their feeding can have devastating effects on victims, sometimes causing permanent memory loss if multiple leeches feed on the same individual.

Scholars of the Veiled Library study these creatures to better understand the nature of memory and the Veil itself. Some mages have attempted to domesticate memory leeches to use in memory-altering spells or to recover lost memories, though such efforts are fraught with risk.

Encounter Ideas

  • A cluster of memory leeches has taken up residence in an abandoned library, feeding on the memories contained in personal journals and diaries.
  • A noble hires the party to capture a memory leech intact, hoping to use it to recover memories lost to a magical accident.
  • Villagers near an ancient battlefield are experiencing strange gaps in their memories, not realizing that memory leeches are emerging from the Veil at night to feed.

Additional Veil entities include:

  • Echoing Shade (CR 4) - Shadowy entities that mimic the actions of creatures they observe
  • Temporal Displacer (CR 6) - Predators that can briefly shift their targets through time
  • Forgotten One (CR 8) - Powerful entities formed from collective forgotten memories
  • Memory Weaver (CR 5) - Spiders that create webs from memories and can manipulate them
  • Veil Walker (CR 7) - Humanoid entities that can move freely between the Material Plane and the Veil

Corrupted Beings

These once-normal creatures have been twisted by exposure to magical corruption, often as a result of environmental degradation. They represent the physical manifestation of the environmental crisis facing Verdantis.

Blightwood Shambler

Large plant, chaotic neutral

Armor Class: 16 (natural armor)

Hit Points: 95 (10d10 + 40)

Speed: 20 ft.

STR 18 (+4)
DEX 8 (-1)
CON 18 (+4)
INT 10 (+0)
WIS 12 (+1)
CHA 8 (-1)

Skills: Perception +4

Damage Vulnerabilities: Fire

Damage Resistances: Bludgeoning, Piercing

Damage Immunities: Poison

Condition Immunities: Poisoned

Senses: Darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 14

Languages: Understands Sylvan but can't speak

Challenge: 5 (1,800 XP)

Corrupted Nature. The blightwood shambler was once a treant or similar plant creature before being corrupted by magical pollution. It retains fragments of its former consciousness but is driven primarily by pain and rage.

Siege Monster. The shambler deals double damage to objects and structures.

Corruption Aura. At the start of each of the shambler's turns, each creature within 10 feet of it takes 3 (1d6) poison damage. Plant creatures have disadvantage on this saving throw.

Actions

Multiattack. The blightwood shambler makes two slam attacks.

Slam. Melee Weapon Attack: +7 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 13 (2d8 + 4) bludgeoning damage plus 3 (1d6) poison damage.

Corrupting Roots (Recharge 5-6). The shambler causes corrupted roots to burst from the ground in a 15-foot radius around itself. Each creature in that area must succeed on a DC 15 Dexterity saving throw or take 14 (4d6) piercing damage and be restrained by the roots. A creature can be freed if it or another creature uses an action to make a DC 15 Strength check. The roots wither away after 1 minute.

Corruption Spores (1/Day). The shambler releases a cloud of corrupted spores in a 20-foot radius. Each creature in that area must succeed on a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or become poisoned for 1 minute. While poisoned in this way, a creature takes 5 (1d10) poison damage at the start of each of its turns. A creature can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success.

Blightwood Shambler Lore

Blightwood shamblers are the tragic result of magical corruption affecting treants and other sentient plant creatures. When a region's Environmental State drops to 2 or lower, the magical pollution can seep into the very essence of these ancient guardians, twisting them into corrupted versions of their former selves.

These creatures retain fragments of their former consciousness and memories, which makes them all the more tragic. They often experience moments of lucidity where they recognize what they've become, only to be overwhelmed again by pain and rage. Some have been observed trying to return to sacred groves or forests they once protected, now unwittingly spreading corruption to these areas.

Druids of the Verdant Covenant study blightwood shamblers in hopes of finding a way to reverse their corruption. They believe that if the Environmental State of a region can be improved significantly, these creatures might be healed rather than destroyed.

Encounter Ideas

  • A blightwood shambler has returned to a sacred grove it once protected, and local druids are desperately trying to contain it without destroying it.
  • Several shamblers are being drawn to a powerful ley line nexus, where their corruption could spread to the entire network if not stopped.
  • A shambler retains enough of its former self to seek help, approaching settlements despite knowing it will be attacked.

Additional corrupted beings include:

  • Corruption Elemental (CR 6) - Elementals formed from concentrated magical pollution
  • Blightfang Predator (CR 4) - Corrupted wolves or big cats with acidic saliva
  • Tainted Dryad (CR 3) - Once-beautiful forest spirits now spreading corruption
  • Sludge Amalgam (CR 5) - Amorphous creatures formed from multiple corrupted animals
  • Withering Sprite (CR 1/2) - Tiny fey corrupted by magical pollution

Cult Members

Various cults and organizations exploit or worship the corruption spreading through Verdantis. These humanoid adversaries represent ideological opposition to environmental restoration efforts.

Arcane Exploitation Extractor

Medium humanoid (any race), lawful evil

Armor Class: 13 (leather armor)

Hit Points: 39 (6d8 + 12)

Speed: 30 ft.

STR 10 (+0)
DEX 14 (+2)
CON 14 (+2)
INT 16 (+3)
WIS 12 (+1)
CHA 10 (+0)

Skills: Arcana +5, Investigation +5, Nature +5

Damage Resistances: Poison

Senses: passive Perception 11

Languages: Common plus any two languages

Challenge: 3 (700 XP)

Corruption Resistance. The extractor has advantage on saving throws against being poisoned and against effects related to corrupted environments.

Ley Line Sense. The extractor can sense the presence of ley lines within 60 feet and can determine their relative strength and direction.

Spellcasting. The extractor is a 4th-level spellcaster. Its spellcasting ability is Intelligence (spell save DC 13, +5 to hit with spell attacks). The extractor has the following wizard spells prepared:

  • Cantrips (at will): acid splash, mage hand, prestidigitation
  • 1st level (4 slots): detect magic, magic missile, shield
  • 2nd level (3 slots): hold person, misty step

Actions

Extraction Staff. Melee Weapon Attack: +2 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 2 (1d6-1) bludgeoning damage plus 7 (2d6) poison damage.

Ley Line Tap (Recharge 5-6). The extractor uses their specialized equipment to tap into a nearby ley line. Each creature in a 15-foot cone must make a DC 13 Constitution saving throw, taking 14 (4d6) force damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one. If used in an area with Environmental State 4 or lower, the Environmental State temporarily decreases by 1 for 1d4 hours.

Corruption Vial. The extractor throws a vial of concentrated corruption at a point within 30 feet. Each creature in a 10-foot-radius sphere centered on that point must succeed on a DC 13 Dexterity saving throw or take 10 (3d6) poison damage and be poisoned until the end of the extractor's next turn. The extractor carries 2 such vials.

Arcane Exploitation Extractor Lore

Arcane Exploitation Extractors are specialized technicians employed by the Arcane Exploitation League to identify, tap into, and drain magical energy from ley lines with little regard for environmental consequences. They combine arcane knowledge with specialized equipment to maximize extraction efficiency.

These individuals are often recruited from academic institutions, lured by promises of unrestricted research opportunities and substantial compensation. Many begin their careers believing they're advancing magical science, only to gradually become desensitized to the environmental damage they cause.

Extractors undergo modifications that allow them to work in corrupted environments without suffering immediate effects, though long-term exposure still takes its toll. Senior extractors often show signs of corruption themselves—discolored veins, minor mutations, or unusual magical resonances.

The Arcane Exploitation League deploys extractors to newly discovered ley line sites, often with mercenary protection. They establish extraction operations that, if left unchecked, can reduce a region's Environmental State by 2-3 points within months.

Encounter Ideas

  • A team of extractors is conducting a preliminary survey of a pristine forest, marking ley line nodes for future exploitation.
  • An extractor has gone rogue, stealing equipment and research to sell to a rival organization.
  • A veteran extractor, suffering from advanced corruption effects, seeks help from the party to escape the League and atone for their actions.

Additional cult members include:

  • Corruption Cultist (CR 1) - Fanatical worshippers who believe corruption is the next stage of evolution
  • League Enforcer (CR 2) - Mercenaries hired to protect Arcane Exploitation League operations
  • Void Channeler (CR 4) - Spellcasters who draw power directly from corruption
  • Blight Shepherd (CR 3) - Corrupted druids who believe nature must be "cleansed" through corruption
  • Corporate Negotiator (CR 1/2) - Silver-tongued representatives who bribe and manipulate communities

Environmental Guardians

These creatures protect the natural world of Verdantis. While they may initially be neutral or even hostile to characters, they can become powerful allies for those committed to environmental restoration.

Verdant Sentinel

Large elemental, neutral good

Armor Class: 16 (natural armor)

Hit Points: 114 (12d10 + 48)

Speed: 30 ft.

STR 18 (+4)
DEX 14 (+2)
CON 18 (+4)
INT 12 (+1)
WIS 16 (+3)
CHA 14 (+2)

Skills: Nature +4, Perception +6

Damage Resistances: Bludgeoning, Piercing, and Slashing from Nonmagical Attacks

Damage Immunities: Poison

Condition Immunities: Exhaustion, Paralyzed, Petrified, Poisoned, Unconscious

Senses: Darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 16

Languages: Sylvan, Druidic, understands Common but rarely speaks it

Challenge: 7 (2,900 XP)

Environmental Attunement. The sentinel is magically bound to a specific natural area. While within this area, it has advantage on attack rolls, ability checks, and saving throws. The sentinel knows if its bound area is threatened or damaged.

Magic Resistance. The sentinel has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects.

Regeneration. The sentinel regains 10 hit points at the start of its turn if it has at least 1 hit point and is in an area with Environmental State 5 or higher. If it is in an area with Environmental State 4 or lower, this trait doesn't function.

Innate Spellcasting. The sentinel's innate spellcasting ability is Wisdom (spell save DC 14). It can innately cast the following spells, requiring no material components:

  • At will: druidcraft, speak with plants
  • 3/day each: entangle, plant growth
  • 1/day each: wall of thorns, commune with nature

Actions

Multiattack. The sentinel makes two Vine Slam attacks.

Vine Slam. Melee Weapon Attack: +7 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 13 (2d8 + 4) bludgeoning damage.

Thorn Spray (Recharge 5-6). The sentinel releases a spray of magical thorns in a 30-foot cone. Each creature in that area must make a DC 14 Dexterity saving throw, taking 21 (6d6) piercing damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one.

Healing Touch (3/Day). The sentinel touches a plant creature or a patch of land no larger than 30 feet square. If it touches a plant creature, that creature regains 21 (6d6) hit points. If it touches land, the Environmental State of that area increases by 1 (maximum 10) for 24 hours.

Reactions

Protective Growth. When a creature the sentinel can see within 30 feet of it is hit by an attack, the sentinel can cause thick vegetation to spring up around that creature, granting it a +2 bonus to AC against that attack. If this causes the attack to miss, vines and roots momentarily entangle the attacker, reducing its speed by half until the end of its next turn.

Verdant Sentinel Lore

Verdant Sentinels are elemental guardians created by powerful nature spirits or circles of druids to protect areas of significant environmental importance. They are composed of living plant matter infused with elemental energy and bound to a specific location such as an ancient grove, a powerful ley line nexus, or a rare ecosystem.

These guardians are intelligent and discerning, able to distinguish between those who mean harm to their protected areas and those who are merely passing through. They often observe visitors for some time before revealing themselves, judging intentions through actions rather than words.

Sentinels are deeply connected to the Environmental State of their bound regions. In healthy environments, they are vibrant and powerful, while in corrupted areas, they become weakened and may even enter dormant states to preserve their energy until the land can be healed.

The Verdant Covenant works closely with these entities, sometimes helping to create new sentinels for threatened areas. A sentinel can exist for centuries, developing unique personalities and accumulating vast knowledge about the history and ecology of their regions.

Encounter Ideas

  • A sentinel tests the party's commitment to environmental protection before allowing them to access an ancient shrine hidden in its protected grove.
  • A weakened sentinel seeks help to drive off Arcane Exploitation League extractors who have begun operations in its territory.
  • The party discovers a dormant sentinel in a heavily corrupted area and must find a way to revitalize it to gain its ancient knowledge.

Additional environmental guardians include:

  • Ley Line Warden (CR 5) - Humanoid manifestations of ley line energy
  • Restoration Sprite (CR 1/4) - Tiny fey that heal small areas of corruption
  • Ancient Treant (CR 9) - Massive, ancient treants that guard the oldest forests
  • Purification Elemental (CR 6) - Water elementals with the ability to cleanse corruption
  • Verdant Drake (CR 8) - Dragon-like creatures that protect wilderness areas

Using Monsters in Your Campaign

Encounter Building

When designing encounters with the creatures in this bestiary, consider the following:

  • Environment: Many Homesalid monsters have abilities that interact with their native environments. Use these to create more dynamic and challenging encounters.
  • Themes: Select monsters that reinforce the themes of your campaign or adventure. For example, Veil entities work well in stories about lost knowledge or forgotten history.
  • Factions: Consider which factions might use, study, or hunt certain monsters. This can create interesting three-way conflicts or unexpected alliances.
  • Scaling: The statistics provided are baselines. Feel free to adjust hit points, damage, or abilities to challenge your specific party.

Monster Lore and Discovery

In Homesalid, knowledge about monsters is not always widely available. Consider using the following methods to allow characters to discover information about creatures they encounter:

  • Intelligence (Nature) or (Arcana) checks to recall basic information
  • Research in libraries or consultation with scholars
  • Forgotten memories that provide insight into creatures connected to the Veil
  • Local folklore that may contain truth mixed with superstition

Revealing monster information gradually can create memorable moments of discovery and give knowledge-focused characters opportunities to shine.
