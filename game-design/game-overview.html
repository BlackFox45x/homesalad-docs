Game Overview

This section provides a high-level overview of HomeSalad, including its core concept, target audience, unique selling points, and gameplay pillars.

Core Concept

HomeSalad is a community-building RPG with gardening mechanics and environmental activism themes. Players take on the role of a newcomer to Greenville, a town facing economic hardship, environmental degradation, and corporate takeover threats. Through sustainable practices, community organizing, and environmental restoration, players work to transform Greenville into a thriving, resilient community.

The game combines elements of farming simulators, town-building games, and narrative RPGs to create a unique experience centered around sustainability, community resilience, and environmental stewardship.

Target Audience

HomeSalad is designed to appeal to several overlapping player demographics:

  • Farming/Life Sim Enthusiasts: Players who enjoy games like Stardew Valley, Animal Crossing, and Harvest Moon
  • Narrative RPG Players: Those who appreciate character-driven stories and meaningful choices
  • Environmental Enthusiasts: People interested in sustainability, permaculture, and environmental issues
  • Community Builders: Players who enjoy social simulation and community management games
  • Educational Gamers: Those looking to learn about sustainable practices through engaging gameplay

The game is designed to be accessible to casual players while offering depth for those who want to engage more deeply with its systems and themes.

Unique Selling Points

Environmental Transformation System

Unlike most games where the environment is static or changes only through scripted events, HomeSalad features a dynamic environmental system where player actions directly and visibly transform the landscape. Areas progress from degraded to restored states through player interventions, with visual feedback, gameplay benefits, and narrative recognition.

Community as Character

The town of Greenville functions as a character that evolves throughout the game. The community has its own metrics, personality, and development arc that responds to player actions. Rather than focusing solely on individual relationships, HomeSalad emphasizes collective transformation and social dynamics.

Integrated Knowledge Systems

HomeSalad incorporates multiple knowledge systems (traditional ecological knowledge, scientific understanding, practical skills, and systems thinking) that players can learn and integrate. This approach respects diverse ways of knowing and encourages players to combine insights from different perspectives.

Collaborative Multiplayer

The game supports multiplayer collaboration where players work together to transform Greenville. Unlike competitive multiplayer games, HomeSalad emphasizes cooperation, resource sharing, and collective achievement, reflecting its themes of community resilience.

Gameplay Pillars

HomeSalad is built around four core gameplay pillars that inform all design decisions:

Pillar Description Key Mechanics
Environmental Stewardship Caring for and restoring the natural environment through sustainable practices Gardening, ecosystem restoration, pollution cleanup, biodiversity enhancement
Community Building Developing relationships, organizing collective action, and strengthening social bonds Relationship system, community events, collective projects, faction reputation
Knowledge & Skills Learning, applying, and sharing various forms of knowledge and practical skills Skill progression, knowledge collection, teaching mechanics, experimentation
Sustainable Systems Creating and maintaining interconnected systems that support long-term resilience Resource cycles, energy management, waste reduction, circular economy

Game Structure

HomeSalad is structured around a three-year narrative arc, with each year representing a deeper level of community transformation:

Year One: Foundations

Players establish themselves in Greenville, begin small-scale projects, form initial relationships, and learn basic skills. The community is fragmented, environmental degradation is severe, and MegaGrow's influence is growing.

Year Two: Growth

Projects expand in scale and impact, community organization strengthens, and direct confrontation with MegaGrow intensifies. Players implement larger systems, develop specialized skills, and begin to see significant environmental recovery.

Year Three: Transformation

The community reaches a critical point of change, with the final confrontation with MegaGrow and the potential for complete transformation of Greenville. Players' choices throughout the game determine the nature of this transformation and the future of the community.

After completing the main narrative arc, players can continue in an open-ended mode, further developing Greenville and potentially expanding their influence to neighboring communities.

Platform & Technical Specifications

  • Platforms: PC (Windows, Mac, Linux), with potential console ports
  • Engine: Unity with uMMORPG Remastered framework
  • Perspective: Third-person with adjustable camera
  • Art Style: Stylized realism with vibrant colors and clear visual language
  • Multiplayer: Optional cooperative play for up to 4 players
  • Accessibility: Comprehensive accessibility features (detailed in Accessibility section)

Design Note: HomeSalad is designed to balance engaging gameplay with meaningful themes. While it addresses serious environmental and social issues, it maintains an optimistic tone that emphasizes the possibility of positive change through collective action.
