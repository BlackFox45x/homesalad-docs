Garden Building System

This section details HomeSalad's garden building system, including garden types, component progression, design patterns, and the evolution of garden capabilities throughout the game.

Garden Building Overview

The garden building system is a core gameplay mechanic that allows players to design, construct, and evolve various types of growing spaces. Gardens progress from simple plots to complex integrated systems as players gain knowledge, skills, and resources.

Specialization Branches

The garden progression tree branches into four main specializations:

Food Production Branch

Focuses on maximizing food yield and efficiency

Row Garden (Tier 1)

Description: Traditional rows of single crops with paths between

Benefits: Simple to plan, easy maintenance access

Unlocks: Basic vegetable production, simple crop rotation

Raised Beds (Tier 2)

Description: Elevated growing areas with defined borders

Benefits: Improved soil control, better drainage, ergonomic access

Unlocks: 25% yield increase, season extension options

Intensive Beds (Tier 3)

Description: Densely planted beds with companion planting

Benefits: Maximum space utilization, reduced weeding

Unlocks: 50% yield increase, companion planting bonuses

Biointensive System (Tier 4)

Description: Deep soil preparation with closed-loop fertility

Benefits: Maximum yield per area, soil building

Unlocks: 100% yield increase, 50% water reduction

Market Garden (Tier 5)

Description: Professional-level food production system

Benefits: Maximum efficiency, crop diversity, year-round production

Unlocks: 200% yield increase, premium crop quality, commercial-scale production

Permaculture Systems Branch

Focuses on self-sustaining ecological systems

Herb Spiral (Tier 1)

Description: Spiral-shaped raised bed with microclimate zones

Benefits: Multiple growing conditions in small space

Unlocks: Microclimate creation, herb variety

Keyhole Garden (Tier 2)

Description: Circular bed with access notch and central compost

Benefits: Integrated composting, water efficiency

Unlocks: 30% water reduction, continuous fertility

Hugelkultur (Tier 3)

Description: Mound system with buried wood and organic matter

Benefits: Long-term fertility, water retention

Unlocks: 50% water reduction, no-irrigation options

Guild System (Tier 4)

Description: Plant communities with complementary functions

Benefits: Self-maintaining, multiple yields, pest resistance

Unlocks: 75% maintenance reduction, ecosystem services

Food Forest (Tier 5)

Description: Multi-layered perennial system mimicking forest structure

Benefits: Self-sustaining, maximum diversity, minimal maintenance

Unlocks: 90% maintenance reduction, continuous harvests, maximum biodiversity

Ornamental Gardens Branch

Focuses on aesthetic and community wellbeing benefits

Specialized Production Branch

Garden Components

Gardens are built from various components that unlock as players progress:

Bed Types

Ground Level Bed

Tier: 1

Materials: Soil amendments only

Benefits: No construction needed, unlimited size

Limitations: Poor drainage, soil quality issues, pest access

Raised Bed

Tier: 2

Materials: Wood, stone, or brick + quality soil

Benefits: Better drainage, soil control, reduced bending

Limitations: Material cost, size constraints, eventual replacement

Wicking Bed

Tier: 3

Materials: Liner, gravel, pipe, soil layers

Benefits: Water efficiency, drought resistance

Limitations: Complex construction, higher cost

Hugelkultur Bed

Tier: 3

Materials: Logs, branches, compost, soil layers

Benefits: Long-term fertility, water retention

Limitations: Large space requirement, initial decomposition period

Aquaponic Bed

Tier: 4

Materials: Waterproof container, grow media, plumbing

Benefits: Fish integration, high productivity, water efficiency

Limitations: Technical complexity, energy requirements

Water Management

Hand Watering

Tier: 1

Materials: Watering can or hose

Benefits: Precise control, no installation

Limitations: Time-consuming, inconsistent

Soaker Hose

Tier: 2

Materials: Porous hose, connectors

Benefits: Water conservation, root-level moisture

Limitations: Limited configuration options

Drip Irrigation

Tier: 3

Materials: Tubing, emitters, connectors, timer

Benefits: Precise delivery, automation, water efficiency

Limitations: Setup complexity, maintenance

Rainwater Harvesting

Tier: 3

Materials: Barrels/tanks, gutters, filters, pump

Benefits: Free water source, sustainability

Limitations: Weather dependence, storage space

Greywater System

Tier: 4

Materials: Filtration components, distribution system

Benefits: Water recycling, reduced consumption

Limitations: Complex regulations, filtration requirements

Garden Design Patterns

Players can implement various design patterns to optimize their gardens:

Keyhole Pattern

  • Planning Mode: Grid-based layout planning before committing resources

  • Seasonal Preview: Visualization of the garden across different seasons

Garden Evolution Mechanics

Gardens evolve over time through several mechanics:

Soil Development

  • Initial State: Basic soil with limited fertility and structure
  • Improvement Methods: Composting, cover cropping, mulching, amendments
  • Evolution Stages: Poor → Basic → Good → Rich → Living Soil
  • Visual Changes: Color darkens, texture changes, visible organic matter
  • Gameplay Effects: Increased yields, reduced water needs, pest resistance

Plant Establishment

  • Annual Cycles: Seasonal planting, growth, harvest, and renewal
  • Perennial Growth: Multi-year establishment with increasing yields
  • Self-Seeding: Plants begin to reproduce naturally in later stages
  • Visual Changes: Increased size, density, and complexity of plantings
  • Gameplay Effects: Reduced maintenance, continuous harvests

System Integration

  • Component Connections: Water systems, compost, animal integration
  • Ecological Relationships: Beneficial insects, soil life, plant communities
  • Evolution Stages: Isolated → Connected → Integrated → Synergistic
  • Visual Changes: Increased biodiversity, visible ecosystem activity
  • Gameplay Effects: Self-regulation, multiple yields, resilience

Design Note: The garden building system is designed to reward both immediate creativity and long-term investment. Players can see quick results from their designs while also experiencing the satisfaction of gardens that mature and evolve over multiple in-game years.
