Quest & Reward System

This section details HomeSalad's quest and reward systems, including quest progression, reward types, leveling mechanics, and how these systems integrate with character development.

Quest Progression System

HomeSalad's quest system is structured to provide a clear sense of progression while maintaining player freedom and supporting multiple playstyles.

AI Prompt: "A flowchart diagram showing quest progression in a community-building game. The diagram shows a central 'Main Story' path with three years/chapters, with multiple branches for faction quests, personal quests, and community projects. Show how these paths interconnect, with decision points and alternative routes. Use different colors for different quest types and include small icons representing quest outcomes."

Quest Tiers

Quests are organized into five tiers of increasing complexity and impact:

Tier Description Examples Typical Rewards
Tier 1
(Introduction)		 Simple tasks to introduce basic gameplay mechanics and characters Planting first garden, meeting neighbors, basic harvesting Basic tools, seeds, small knowledge gains
Tier 2
(Establishment)		 More complex tasks requiring basic skills and resource management Building garden beds, community introductions, simple events Improved tools, uncommon seeds, skill experience
Tier 3
(Development)		 Multi-stage quests requiring planning and skill application Neighborhood projects, faction introductions, specialized gardens Specialized equipment, rare seeds, blueprint unlocks
Tier 4
(Challenge)		 Complex quests with significant choices and consequences Major community projects, faction conflicts, environmental challenges Unique items, land access, relationship milestones
Tier 5
(Culmination)		 Major storyline events with far-reaching impact on the game world Seasonal festivals, corporate confrontations, community transformation Transformative upgrades, permanent world changes, legacy items

Quest Tracks

Quests are organized into parallel tracks that players can progress through simultaneously:

Main Storyline

The central narrative of HomeSalad follows the community's three-year journey to transform Greenville and confront MegaGrow Industries.

Year 1: Establishment

Focus: Setting up initial gardens, meeting key community members, learning about Greenville's challenges

Culmination: First Harvest Festival with community recognition

Key Rewards: Home base expansion, core tool set, community standing

Year 2: Development

Focus: Expanding influence, addressing MegaGrow's plans, building community resilience

Culmination: City Council showdown over land use policies

Key Rewards: Access to new areas, advanced systems, political influence

Year 3: Transformation

Focus: Implementing community vision, final confrontation with MegaGrow, securing Greenville's future

Culmination: Greenville Transformation Celebration

Key Rewards: Legacy items, permanent world changes, epilogue content

Faction Questlines

Each faction offers a questline that reflects its values and goals, with reputation-based progression.

Introduction (Neutral)

Focus: Meeting faction members, learning about their philosophy

Gating: Available upon discovering the faction

Key Rewards: Basic faction-specific items, initial reputation

Assistance (Friendly)

Focus: Helping with faction projects, demonstrating alignment

Gating: Requires Friendly reputation level

Key Rewards: Specialized techniques, faction-specific blueprints

Collaboration (Allied)

Focus: Deep involvement in faction activities, representing faction interests

Gating: Requires Allied reputation level

Key Rewards: Unique faction items, special abilities, faction perks

Leadership (Trusted)

Focus: Guiding faction direction, resolving internal conflicts

Gating: Requires Trusted reputation level

Key Rewards: Faction leadership role, exclusive content, legacy impact

Personal Questlines

Character-specific quests that develop relationships with individual NPCs.

Community Projects

Collaborative endeavors that improve Greenville's infrastructure and environment.

Seasonal Events

Time-limited quests tied to the seasonal cycle and calendar events.

Reward System

HomeSalad's reward system is designed to provide meaningful progression while maintaining balance and supporting multiple playstyles.

Reward Categories

Quests provide various types of rewards that contribute to different aspects of progression:

Material Rewards

  • Tools: Equipment that enables new activities or improves efficiency
  • Seeds: New plant varieties with different properties and uses
  • Resources: Building materials, soil amendments, crafting ingredients
  • Unique Items: Special objects with rare properties or functions
  • Currency: Community credits for trading and purchasing
Tool Progression Example
Tier Tool Properties Acquisition
1 Basic Watering Can Small capacity, slow watering Starting equipment
2 Copper Watering Can Medium capacity, moderate speed Tier 2 community quest reward
3 Rainwater Watering Can Large capacity, plant health bonus Green Spark Collective faction quest
4 Precision Watering Can Adjustable flow, water conservation Science Institute faction quest
5 Living Water Can Self-filling, growth acceleration Year 3 main quest reward

Knowledge Rewards

  • Techniques: New methods for gardening, building, or crafting
  • Blueprints: Designs for structures, garden layouts, or systems
  • Recipes: Formulas for creating food, medicine, or crafted items
  • Lore: Background information about the world and characters
  • Skill Experience: Direct advancement in specific skill areas

Social Rewards

  • Reputation: Improved standing with factions and groups
  • Relationships: Deeper connections with individual characters
  • Community Status: Recognition and influence in Greenville
  • Access: Entry to restricted areas or exclusive events
  • Assistance: Help from NPCs with various activities

Environmental Rewards

  • Land Access: New areas for gardening and building
  • Environmental Healing: Visible improvements to degraded areas
  • Infrastructure: Community improvements like water systems
  • Biodiversity: New plant and animal species in the environment
  • Climate Effects: Positive changes to local weather patterns

Leveling and Progression

HomeSalad uses a multi-faceted progression system rather than traditional character levels:

Community Influence

The primary measure of overall progression is Community Influence, which reflects the player's impact on Greenville.

AI Prompt: "A progression chart showing 10 levels of 'Community Influence' in a community-building game. Each level shows the influence points required, title earned (like 'Newcomer' to 'Community Leader'), and key benefits unlocked. Use a clean, infographic style with icons representing each benefit type."

Influence Level Title Points Required Key Unlocks
1 Newcomer 0 Basic tools, home garden plot, introductory quests
2 Neighbor 100 Community garden access, basic blueprints, neighborhood quests
3 Community Member 300 Home expansion, faction introductions, community board access
4 Local Advocate 600 Workshop access, advanced blueprints, district projects
5 Respected Voice 1000 City Council access, rare seeds, leadership opportunities
6 Community Organizer 1500 Additional land, specialized equipment, policy influence
7 Local Leader 2200 District representation, unique blueprints, mentor abilities
8 Town Visionary 3000 City-wide projects, legacy planning, advanced systems
9 Community Pillar 4000 Major infrastructure, rare techniques, festival planning
10 Greenville Legend 5500 City transformation options, legendary items, endgame content

Influence Acquisition

Players gain Community Influence through various activities:

  • Quest Completion: Primary source of influence points
  • Environmental Improvement: Transforming degraded areas
  • Community Projects: Contributing to shared initiatives
  • Relationship Building: Developing connections with NPCs
  • Knowledge Sharing: Teaching skills to community members
  • Sustainable Practices: Demonstrating ecological principles

Parallel Progression Systems

In addition to Community Influence, players progress through several parallel systems:

  • Skill Advancement: Individual skills progress through practice (see Skill Tree)
  • Faction Reputation: Standing with each faction has its own progression track
  • NPC Relationships: Individual relationships develop through interaction
  • Environmental Transformation: Areas progress through stages of ecological health
  • Knowledge Collection: Encyclopedia of discovered information expands

Quest and Reward Interface

The quest and reward systems are presented through several interface elements:

AI Prompt: "A UI design for a quest journal interface in a community-building game. Show a page with a selected quest displaying objectives, rewards, and progress. Include a quest list on the left side categorized by type (main story, faction, personal, etc.) with color coding and status indicators. Use a natural paper/journal aesthetic with handwritten-style fonts and botanical decorative elements."

For details on how the quest journal and reward interfaces are implemented in the game's technical architecture, refer to the UI MCP Implementation documentation, which covers the integration with the game's networking and data systems.

Quest Journal

  • Quest Tracking: List of active, available, and completed quests
  • Objective Display: Current steps and progress for active quests
  • Reward Preview: Information about potential quest rewards
  • Quest Map: Spatial representation of quest locations
  • Quest History: Record of completed quests and choices made

Community Board

  • Available Quests: New opportunities posted by community members
  • Project Status: Progress of ongoing community initiatives
  • Event Calendar: Upcoming time-limited activities and quests
  • Request System: Player-initiated help requests for multiplayer
  • Achievement Display: Community accomplishments and milestones

Influence Panel

  • Current Level: Visual representation of Community Influence
  • Progress Tracking: Bar showing advancement toward next level
  • Recent Gains: Log of recently acquired influence points
  • Unlocked Benefits: List of capabilities gained through influence
  • Future Previews: Upcoming rewards for continued progression

Design Note: The quest and reward systems are designed to create a sense of meaningful progression without relying on traditional RPG leveling mechanics. By tying advancement to community impact rather than combat or grinding, the game reinforces its themes of cooperation, sustainability, and positive change.
