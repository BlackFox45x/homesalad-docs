Character Progression

This section details the character progression systems in HomeSalad, including skills, knowledge, relationships, and reputation mechanics that allow players to develop their character throughout the game.

Progression Philosophy

Character progression in HomeSalad is designed around several core principles:

  • Learning by Doing: Skills improve primarily through practice and application rather than abstract point allocation
  • Multiple Progression Paths: Players can advance through various complementary systems rather than a single linear path
  • Meaningful Choices: Specialization requires investment and trade-offs, making character build decisions significant
  • Visible Impact: Progression visibly affects the game world, character capabilities, and social dynamics
  • Community Connection: Individual progression is tied to community development, emphasizing interdependence

Skill System

The skill system represents the player character's practical abilities and technical knowledge. Skills improve through use and application in relevant activities.

Skill Categories

Category Skills Primary Activities
Cultivation Gardening, Composting, Seed Saving, Pest Management, Plant Breeding Growing food, managing soil health, developing plant varieties
Construction Building, Crafting, Tool Making, Salvaging, Design Creating structures, tools, and infrastructure
Community Leadership, Teaching, Negotiation, Event Planning, Conflict Resolution Organizing people, sharing knowledge, building relationships
Conservation Ecosystem Restoration, Water Management, Wildlife Support, Pollution Remediation Restoring natural systems and environmental health
Crafts Cooking, Preservation, Textiles, Herbalism, Artistry Creating useful and beautiful items from natural materials

Skill Levels

Each skill progresses through five levels of mastery:

  1. Novice: Basic understanding and capability, occasional failures, limited efficiency
  2. Apprentice: Reliable performance of standard techniques, improved resource efficiency
  3. Practitioner: Expanded techniques, good resource efficiency, ability to teach basics
  4. Expert: Advanced techniques, excellent resource efficiency, ability to innovate
  5. Master: Complete mastery, maximum efficiency, ability to create unique solutions

Skill Progression Mechanics

Skills improve through several mechanisms:

  • Practice: Performing activities related to the skill (primary method)
  • Learning: Reading books, attending workshops, or being taught by NPCs
  • Experimentation: Trying new approaches or combinations of techniques
  • Teaching: Sharing knowledge with NPCs or other players
  • Challenges: Completing specific skill-related tasks or quests

Skill progression follows a curve where early levels are achieved relatively quickly, while mastery requires significant dedication and practice.

Knowledge System

The knowledge system represents information, insights, and understanding that the player character accumulates. Unlike skills, knowledge is not measured in levels but as discrete pieces of information that can be collected, connected, and applied.

Knowledge Categories

  • Ecological: Information about local ecosystems, species interactions, and environmental processes
  • Technical: Specific techniques, recipes, blueprints, and methodologies
  • Cultural: Local history, traditions, stories, and community knowledge
  • Personal: Information about specific characters, their preferences, histories, and relationships
  • Systemic: Understanding of how different systems interact and affect each other

Knowledge Acquisition

Players can gain knowledge through:

  • Conversations with NPCs
  • Reading books and documents
  • Observation of natural phenomena
  • Experimentation and testing
  • Attending community events and workshops
  • Completing quests and challenges

Knowledge Web

Knowledge in HomeSalad is represented as an interconnected web rather than a linear progression. As players discover new information, they can connect it to existing knowledge to unlock insights, new dialogue options, and special actions.

The Knowledge Web is visualized in the player's journal, showing connections between different pieces of information and highlighting areas where knowledge is incomplete.

Relationship System

The relationship system tracks the player's connections with individual NPCs and factions, reflecting how their actions and choices affect their standing in the community.

Individual Relationships

Relationships with individual NPCs are measured along three dimensions:

  • Trust: How much the NPC believes in the player's reliability and intentions
  • Respect: How much the NPC values the player's skills and accomplishments
  • Affinity: How much the NPC personally likes and connects with the player

These dimensions can develop independently based on player actions. For example, an NPC might respect the player's gardening skills while not fully trusting their intentions, or might have high affinity but low respect.

Relationship Development

Relationships develop through:

  • Dialogue choices that reveal character values and perspectives
  • Completing personal quests for NPCs
  • Including NPCs in community projects
  • Regular interactions and activities
  • Gift-giving based on NPC preferences
  • Supporting (or opposing) NPC goals and values

Relationship Benefits

Strong relationships provide:

  • Access to unique skills and knowledge
  • Special items and resources
  • Additional quest opportunities
  • Support during community events and challenges
  • Deeper narrative content and character development
  • Assistance with projects and tasks

Faction Reputation

Faction reputation represents the player's standing with the major groups in Greenville. Each faction has its own values, goals, and evaluation criteria.

Reputation Levels

  1. Hostile: Faction actively works against player, blocks access to resources
  2. Suspicious: Limited interaction, higher costs for services, restricted information
  3. Neutral: Basic services available, standard pricing, no special benefits
  4. Friendly: Discounted services, occasional assistance, some exclusive options
  5. Allied: Full access to faction resources, regular support, unique quests and rewards
  6. Leadership: Ability to direct faction activities, represent in negotiations, maximum benefits

Building Reputation

Players can improve their standing with factions through:

  • Completing faction-specific quests
  • Supporting faction interests in council meetings
  • Developing projects that align with faction values
  • Building personal relationships with faction members
  • Trading resources valued by the faction
  • Defending faction interests against opponents

Note: Improving reputation with one faction may damage relationships with opposing factions. Players must make strategic choices about which alliances to prioritize.

Character Customization

Beyond skills and relationships, players can customize their character through several systems:

Appearance

  • Physical Traits: Body type, skin tone, facial features, hair style/color
  • Clothing: Outfits with functional benefits (weather protection, tool storage) and aesthetic options
  • Accessories: Hats, backpacks, tools, and decorative items

Specializations

As players advance in skills, they can choose specializations that provide unique abilities and bonuses:

Skill Category Specializations Special Abilities
Cultivation Master Gardener, Plant Breeder, Soil Specialist Unique crop varieties, superior soil fertility, reduced water needs
Construction Architect, Craftsperson, Salvage Expert Unique building designs, high-quality tools, efficient material recovery
Community Leader, Mediator, Educator Improved group coordination, conflict resolution, knowledge sharing
Conservation Ecologist, Water Steward, Restoration Expert Enhanced biodiversity, water purification, accelerated ecosystem recovery
Crafts Chef, Herbalist, Artisan Superior food benefits, medicinal preparations, valuable artistic creations

Home Base

Players can customize their home in Greenville, which serves as both a personal space and a functional base for their activities:

  • Location: Players can choose from available properties in different districts
  • Layout: Interior and exterior spaces can be arranged to suit player preferences
  • Functionality: Specialized areas for crafting, storage, research, and social gatherings
  • Aesthetics: Decorative elements that reflect player style and achievements
  • Sustainability: Systems for energy, water, and waste that reflect player values

Progression Balancing

HomeSalad's progression systems are balanced to provide several key experiences:

Early Game (Year 1)

  • Rapid initial skill development to give players a sense of accomplishment
  • Multiple accessible paths to build initial relationships
  • Clear feedback on how actions affect reputation and relationships
  • Basic knowledge acquisition that introduces key concepts

Mid Game (Year 2)

  • Increasing specialization requirements that encourage strategic choices
  • Deeper relationship development with key NPCs
  • Faction tensions that require balancing competing interests
  • Knowledge connections that reveal deeper systemic insights

Late Game (Year 3)

  • Mastery challenges that test advanced skills
  • Leadership opportunities within factions
  • Resolution of key relationship arcs
  • Integration of knowledge into transformative projects

Design Note: Character progression in HomeSalad is designed to feel organic and integrated with the narrative and world. Rather than abstract "leveling up," advancement is tied to visible changes in the character's capabilities, relationships, and impact on the community.
