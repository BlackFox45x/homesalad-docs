Gameplay Systems

HomeSalad features several interconnected gameplay systems that work together to create a rich, engaging experience centered around community building, environmental restoration, and sustainable living. These systems are designed to be accessible to new players while offering depth and complexity for experienced players.

Core Loop

The core gameplay loop of HomeSalad consists of four main phases that players cycle through as they progress:

Gather

Players collect resources, knowledge, and community support through:

  • Exploring the environment to find materials and seeds
  • Talking with NPCs to learn techniques and gather information
  • Researching sustainable practices at the Science Institute
  • Participating in community events to build relationships
  • Salvaging materials from abandoned structures

Cultivate

Players develop gardens, infrastructure, and relationships through:

  • Designing and planting garden plots
  • Building sustainable infrastructure
  • Nurturing plants through watering, fertilizing, and pest management
  • Developing relationships with community members
  • Creating educational programs and skill-sharing events

Defend

Players protect their projects from threats through:

  • Responding to environmental challenges like storms or droughts
  • Addressing pest invasions and plant diseases
  • Countering corporate interference and land acquisition attempts
  • Building community support to resist harmful policies
  • Creating resilient systems that can withstand challenges

Expand

Players grow their influence and impact through:

  • Restoring new areas of Greenville
  • Scaling up successful projects to community-wide initiatives
  • Forming alliances with other organizations
  • Implementing policy changes through the City Council
  • Tackling larger environmental and social challenges

This loop repeats throughout the game, with increasing complexity and scope as players progress. Early gameplay focuses on small-scale gardening and relationship building, while later gameplay involves community-wide projects, policy changes, and confrontations with MegaGrow Industries.

Gardening System

The gardening system is a core gameplay mechanic that allows players to transform spaces through sustainable agriculture. It combines strategic planning, resource management, and environmental knowledge.

Plot Design

Players can create garden plots of various sizes and configurations, considering factors such as:

  • Sunlight Exposure: Different areas receive varying amounts of sunlight throughout the day, affecting plant growth.
  • Soil Quality: Soil can range from contaminated to rich, with various nutrient profiles and pH levels.
  • Water Access: Proximity to water sources affects irrigation options and water conservation needs.
  • Terrain: Slopes, elevation, and existing features influence drainage and microclimate.
  • Space Efficiency: Strategic layout maximizes yield while maintaining plant health.

Crop Selection

HomeSalad features over 50 plant varieties, each with unique characteristics:

Plant Category Examples Key Features
Vegetables Tomatoes, Carrots, Kale, Peppers, Beans Primary food production, varied growth cycles, diverse nutrient profiles
Fruits Strawberries, Blueberries, Apples, Peaches Perennial options, high value, seasonal harvests
Herbs Basil, Mint, Rosemary, Thyme, Lavender Companion planting benefits, medicinal properties, pest deterrence
Flowers Sunflowers, Marigolds, Zinnias, Nasturtiums Pollinator attraction, pest management, aesthetic value
