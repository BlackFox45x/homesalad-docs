Quest Design

This section details the quest design philosophy, structure, and systems in HomeSalad, including main storyline quests, faction quests, personal quests, and emergent objectives.

Quest Design Philosophy

HomeSalad's quest design is guided by several core principles:

  • Meaningful Choices: Quests present players with decisions that have tangible consequences for the narrative, world, and relationships
  • Systemic Integration: Quests connect to and utilize the game's core systems rather than existing as isolated content
  • Multiple Solutions: Most quests can be approached in different ways based on player skills, relationships, and preferences
  • Environmental Context: Quests are tied to specific locations and environmental conditions, making the setting integral to the experience
  • Community Impact: Quest outcomes visibly affect the community and environment, providing clear feedback on player actions

Quest Categories

HomeSalad features several categories of quests, each with distinct characteristics and purposes:

Main Story Quests

These quests drive the central narrative of HomeSalad, focusing on the conflict with MegaGrow Industries and the transformation of Greenville. They are structured in a three-act format corresponding to the game's three-year cycle.

Key Characteristics:

  • Significant narrative impact with major decision points
  • Involvement of multiple factions and key characters
  • Large-scale environmental and community changes
  • Multi-stage objectives with varied gameplay
  • Memorable set-piece moments and events

Structure: Main story quests follow a branching structure where player choices in earlier quests affect the available options and outcomes in later quests. Each year culminates in a major community event that serves as a narrative climax.

Faction Quests

These quests are associated with specific factions in Greenville and allow players to develop their standing with these groups. Each faction offers a questline that reflects its values, goals, and methods.

Key Characteristics:

  • Alignment with faction values and objectives
  • Reputation rewards and faction-specific benefits
  • Potential conflicts with opposing faction interests
  • Specialized gameplay related to faction focus
  • Character development for faction members

Structure: Faction quests are organized in tiers that unlock as player reputation increases. Higher-tier quests offer more significant rewards and narrative development but may also create more tension with opposing factions.

Personal Quests

These quests focus on individual characters and their personal stories, allowing players to develop deeper relationships with NPCs and help them overcome challenges or achieve goals.

Key Characteristics:

  • Character-driven narratives with emotional depth
  • Relationship development and personal insights
  • Smaller scale but high emotional impact
  • Often tied to character backgrounds and growth
  • Unique rewards including skills, items, or knowledge

Structure: Personal quests typically unfold in 3-5 stages as relationships develop, with each stage revealing more about the character and presenting new challenges or opportunities for connection.

Community Projects

These are collaborative objectives that involve multiple community members working toward a shared goal, such as building infrastructure, organizing events, or addressing environmental challenges.

Key Characteristics:

  • Collective effort requiring resource contribution
  • Visible progress tracking and milestone rewards
  • Community-wide benefits upon completion
  • Opportunities for leadership and coordination
  • Integration with seasonal events and needs

Structure: Community projects have a preparation phase, a resource gathering phase, an implementation phase, and a celebration/benefit phase. Players can contribute at different stages based on their skills and interests.

Emergent Quests

These quests arise dynamically from the game's systems and world state, responding to player actions, environmental conditions, and community needs.

Key Characteristics:

  • Generated based on current game state and conditions
  • Responsive to player actions and environmental changes
  • Often time-limited or seasonal in nature
  • Varied difficulty and scope
  • Integrated with environmental and community systems

Structure: Emergent quests follow templates that are populated with contextually appropriate details based on current conditions, creating unique combinations of objectives, locations, and rewards.

Quest Structure

While quest structure varies by category, most quests in HomeSalad follow a general pattern:

Quest Components

Component Description Example
Initiation How the quest is discovered and started Conversation with NPC, bulletin board notice, environmental observation
Context Background information and motivation Character history, community need, environmental issue
Objectives Tasks that must be completed Gather resources, build structure, convince character, restore area
Choices Decision points that affect outcomes Approach method, resource allocation, ethical dilemmas
Challenges Obstacles that must be overcome Resource scarcity, time pressure, opposition, skill requirements
Resolution Conclusion and outcome Environmental change, relationship development, community impact
Rewards Benefits for completion Skills, knowledge, items, reputation, environmental improvements

Quest Flow

The typical flow of a quest in HomeSalad:

  1. Discovery: Player learns about the quest through exploration, conversation, or events
  2. Investigation: Player gathers information about the situation and possible approaches
  3. Planning: Player decides how to approach the quest based on their skills and resources
  4. Execution: Player carries out their chosen approach, adapting to challenges
  5. Decision Points: Player makes key choices that affect the outcome
  6. Resolution: The quest concludes with consequences based on player actions
  7. Feedback: The world and characters react to the quest outcome
  8. Integration: The results become part of the ongoing game state

Quest Design Examples

The following examples illustrate how HomeSalad's quest design principles are applied to different quest types:

Main Story Quest Example: "Watershed Moment"

Context: MegaGrow plans to divert water from the Green River for their industrial farm, threatening Greenville's water supply and riparian ecosystem.

Objectives:

  • Gather evidence of MegaGrow's water diversion plans
  • Develop a community water management alternative
  • Present the case at the City Council meeting
  • Implement the chosen water management solution

Key Choices:

  • How to obtain evidence (infiltration, legal research, whistleblower)
  • What alternative to propose (traditional methods, high-tech solution, hybrid approach)
  • How to address MegaGrow's arguments (economic, environmental, community-focused)
  • Which allies to involve (affects available resources and political support)

Outcomes: The river's fate is determined, affecting water availability, ecosystem health, and community-corporate relations. The physical environment visibly changes based on the outcome, and water-related gameplay is permanently affected.

Faction Quest Example: "Green Spark Initiative"

Context: The Green Spark Collective wants to establish a demonstration garden in Downtown Commons to showcase sustainable growing methods.

Objectives:

  • Secure permission from the City Council for the garden site
  • Gather appropriate plants and materials
  • Design and build the garden with Collective members
  • Create educational materials about the techniques used
  • Host a public workshop to share knowledge

Key Choices:

  • Garden design focus (food production, native habitat, water conservation)
  • How to approach the Council (formal proposal, public pressure, individual lobbying)
  • Which Collective members to involve (affects available skills and relationships)
  • How to present information (technical, practical, inspirational)

Outcomes: A permanent demonstration garden in Downtown Commons, increased Green Spark reputation, new gardening knowledge, and potential conflict with MegaGrow if the garden contradicts their agricultural messaging.

Personal Quest Example: "Maria's Legacy"

Context: Elder gardener Maria Gonzalez wants to preserve her family's heirloom seed collection but fears it will be lost as younger generations leave Greenville.

Objectives:

  • Help Maria organize and catalog her seed collection
  • Learn the stories and growing techniques for key varieties
  • Find a sustainable way to maintain the collection
  • Connect Maria with younger community members interested in traditional gardening

Key Choices:

  • How to preserve the knowledge (digital documentation, apprenticeship, community seed library)
  • Which varieties to prioritize (based on cultural significance, adaptation to climate change, culinary value)
  • How to engage younger generations (school program, cooking demonstrations, technology integration)

Outcomes: Preserved seed varieties become available to the player, Maria's relationship deepens, traditional knowledge is documented, and a new community tradition may be established.

Quest Tracking and Management

HomeSalad provides several tools for players to track and manage quests:

Journal System

The player's journal serves as the primary quest tracking tool, with several sections:

  • Active Quests: Currently accepted and in-progress quests
  • Completed Quests: Archive of finished quests with outcomes
  • Available Quests: Known but not yet accepted quests
  • Notes: Player-created reminders and observations
  • Calendar: Timeline of upcoming events and deadlines

Quest Markers and Indicators

The game world includes subtle indicators for quest-related elements:

  • Character Indicators: Visual cues for NPCs with available quests or updates
  • Location Highlights: Environmental cues for quest-relevant areas
  • Map Markers: Optional indicators on the world map (can be toggled)
  • Time Indicators: Warnings for time-sensitive objectives

Quest Prioritization

Players can prioritize quests in their journal, which affects:

  • Which quest objectives appear in the HUD
  • Which quest locations are highlighted on the map
  • Which quest-related dialogue options are emphasized
  • Notifications for quest-relevant opportunities

Design Note: Quest design in HomeSalad emphasizes player agency and systemic integration. Rather than treating quests as a separate content layer, they are designed to work with and highlight the game's core systems of environmental transformation, community building, and sustainable living.
