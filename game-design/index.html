HomeSalad Game Design Document

This Game Design Document (GDD) provides comprehensive specifications for HomeSalad's gameplay systems, character progression, quest design, world design, user interface, art direction, and more.

Introduction

HomeSalad is a community-building RPG with gardening mechanics and environmental activism themes. Players transform a struggling town through sustainable practices, progressing from newcomer to community leader and environmental champion.

This Game Design Document details the mechanics, systems, and design principles that will bring the HomeSalad experience to life. It serves as the blueprint for development, ensuring that all gameplay elements support the core vision and narrative themes established in the Story Bible.

Core Gameplay Experience

HomeSalad offers a unique gameplay experience centered around the transformation of both environment and community. The core gameplay loop consists of:

Gather

Players collect resources, knowledge, and community support through exploration, conversation, research, and participation in community events. This phase emphasizes discovery and relationship building.

Cultivate

Using gathered resources, players develop gardens, infrastructure, and relationships. This phase focuses on strategic planning, resource allocation, and system design to create sustainable solutions.

Defend

Players protect their projects from environmental threats (weather events, pests, contamination) and corporate interference. This phase involves problem-solving, community mobilization, and strategic response.

Expand

As players succeed, they grow their influence, restore new areas, and tackle larger challenges. This phase emphasizes scaling solutions, building networks, and creating lasting change in the community.

This loop repeats throughout the game, with increasing complexity and scope as players progress. Early gameplay focuses on small-scale gardening and relationship building, while later gameplay involves community-wide projects, policy changes, and confrontations with MegaGrow Industries.

Key Gameplay Systems

Gardening System

The gardening system is a core gameplay mechanic that allows players to transform spaces through sustainable agriculture. Players design garden plots, select appropriate crops, manage soil health, implement water systems, address pest challenges, and harvest produce.

Key features include:

  • 50+ plant varieties with unique requirements and benefits
  • Seasonal cycles affecting plant growth and selection
  • Soil management through composting, cover crops, and rotation
  • Water conservation through irrigation, rainwater harvesting, and drought-resistant design
  • Organic pest management using companion planting and beneficial insects
  • Value-added processing of harvests into preserved foods, seeds, and crafted items

Community Engagement System

The community engagement system allows players to build relationships, influence factions, organize events, and coordinate volunteers. This system translates individual actions into community-wide impact.

Key features include:

  • Relationship building with NPCs through dialogue, gifts, and assistance
  • Faction standing management affecting resource access and quest availability
  • Community event organization with attendance and outcomes based on player reputation
  • Education programs that spread knowledge and increase community capabilities
  • Volunteer coordination for larger projects requiring collective effort

Environmental Transformation System

The environmental transformation system tracks and visualizes the health of different areas in Greenville. Player actions directly affect environmental metrics, with visible changes to the landscape as areas improve.

Key features include:

  • Environmental state tracking from degraded (0) to fully restored (10)
  • Visual transformation reflecting environmental health
  • Wildlife return based on habitat quality and biodiversity
  • Water and soil quality improvements affecting gameplay options
  • Climate resilience features that reduce impact of extreme weather events

Design Pillars

HomeSalad's design is guided by five core pillars that inform all gameplay systems and mechanics:

Meaningful Choice

Player decisions have visible consequences for the environment, community, and story. Different approaches to challenges offer distinct advantages and trade-offs rather than "right" and "wrong" answers.

Systemic Design

Gameplay elements interact in realistic, interconnected ways. Actions in one system (like gardening) affect others (like community relationships or environmental health), creating emergent gameplay.

Accessible Depth

Systems are easy to engage with initially but offer increasing complexity as players progress. Information is presented progressively to avoid overwhelming new players while satisfying experienced ones.

Tangible Progress

Player advancement is visible in the world through environmental transformation, community evolution, and infrastructure development, not just through abstract statistics.

Transferable Knowledge

Gameplay mechanics are based on real-world principles of sustainability, community organizing, and ecological restoration, allowing players to apply what they learn in their own lives.
