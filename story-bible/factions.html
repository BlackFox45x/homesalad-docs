Factions & Organizations

This section details the various factions and organizations that operate within Greenville, their motivations, relationships, and how they influence the game's narrative and gameplay.

Major Factions Overview

HomeSalad features several major factions that represent different interests and ideologies within Greenville. Players can build relationships with these factions, affecting their standing in the community and unlocking unique gameplay opportunities.

The Green Spark Collective

Leader: Elena Chen (Founder)

Headquarters: The Greenhouse (Downtown Commons)

Ideology: Community-based environmentalism, sustainable living, grassroots activism

Symbol: A green leaf with a spark of light

The Green Spark Collective began as a small group of environmentally-conscious citizens who wanted to revitalize Greenville through sustainable practices. Initially focused on community gardens, they've expanded their vision to include renewable energy, local economy development, and environmental education.

The Collective is loosely organized, with different members specializing in various aspects of sustainability. They operate through consensus decision-making and emphasize skill-sharing and mutual aid. The player can join this faction early in the game and eventually take on a leadership role.

Key Sub-groups:

  • Garden Guild: Focuses on urban agriculture and food production
  • Makers Circle: Creates tools, infrastructure, and upcycled goods
  • Knowledge Keepers: Preserves traditional skills and environmental education
  • Community Organizers: Handles outreach, events, and political advocacy

MegaGrow Industries

Leader: Victor Reynolds (CEO)

Headquarters: Corporate Tower (Outside Greenville, with local office in Downtown)

Ideology: Corporate efficiency, technological solutions, profit-driven development

Symbol: Stylized "MG" with a growth chart arrow

MegaGrow Industries is an agribusiness conglomerate that has been buying up farmland and properties around Greenville. They promote a vision of high-tech, corporate-controlled agriculture and urban development that promises economic revival but threatens local autonomy and environmental health.

MegaGrow represents the primary antagonistic force in the game, though players can choose to work with them to influence their practices from within. The corporation is hierarchical, efficient, and well-funded, with access to advanced technology and political connections.

Key Divisions:

  • AgriTech Division: Develops GMO crops and automated farming systems
  • Urban Development: Plans and implements corporate-style urban renewal
  • Public Relations: Manages community perception and political relationships
  • Research & Development: Creates proprietary agricultural and environmental technologies

Greenville City Council

Leader: Mayor Robert Thompson

Headquarters: City Hall (Downtown Commons)

Ideology: Mixed - different council members represent different interests

Symbol: Greenville city seal (oak tree over water)

The Greenville City Council is the official governing body of the town, responsible for policy decisions, resource allocation, and development approvals. The council is divided between pro-development members who favor MegaGrow's proposals and community advocates who support more grassroots approaches.

The City Council serves as both a battleground for competing visions of Greenville's future and a potential ally for players who engage with local politics. Council decisions directly impact gameplay by approving or blocking projects, allocating public resources, and setting regulations.

Key Positions:

  • Mayor: Breaks ties and represents the city officially
  • Development Committee: Reviews and approves building projects
  • Environmental Commission: Oversees natural resources and pollution control
  • Community Services: Manages public spaces and social programs

Minor Factions

In addition to the major factions, several smaller groups operate within Greenville, each with their own interests and specialties:

Faction Description Relationship to Player
Riverside Elders Association A group of long-time residents who preserve traditional knowledge about farming, crafting, and local history. Based primarily in Riverside Heights. Can provide unique heirloom seeds, traditional techniques, and historical context. Values respect for tradition and community service.
Rust Belt Reclamation Crew Former factory workers who have turned to salvaging and repurposing industrial materials. Operates in the Rust Belt District. Offers access to unique building materials, mechanical expertise, and labor for larger projects. Values practical solutions and worker solidarity.
Brightwater Environmental Coalition Middle-class environmentalists focused on water quality and conservation. Centered in Brightwater Shores. Provides scientific knowledge, water testing equipment, and political connections. Values data-driven approaches and policy advocacy.
Meadowbrook Neighborhood Association Suburban residents concerned with property values and quality of life issues. Based in Meadowbrook Suburbs. Controls access to suburban yards for garden expansion and influences council votes. Values aesthetics, safety, and consensus-building.
Downtown Business Alliance Local business owners seeking economic revitalization while maintaining local character. Centered in Downtown Commons. Offers retail space, marketing opportunities, and economic partnerships. Values local entrepreneurship and distinctive community identity.

Faction Relationships

The relationships between factions are dynamic and can be influenced by player actions:

Initial Faction Relationships

  • Green Spark Collective ↔ MegaGrow Industries: Hostile - Fundamental ideological differences
  • Green Spark Collective ↔ City Council: Cautious - Some allies on the council, but limited influence
  • MegaGrow Industries ↔ City Council: Favorable - Strong economic incentives and political connections
  • Riverside Elders ↔ Green Spark Collective: Supportive - Shared values but generational differences
  • Downtown Business Alliance ↔ MegaGrow: Mixed - Economic opportunities but fears of being replaced
  • Rust Belt Crew ↔ MegaGrow: Hostile - History of labor disputes and factory closures

Faction Reputation System

Player interactions with factions are tracked through a reputation system:

Reputation Levels

  1. Hostile: Faction actively works against player, blocks access to resources
  2. Suspicious: Limited interaction, higher costs for services, restricted information
  3. Neutral: Basic services available, standard pricing, no special benefits
  4. Friendly: Discounted services, occasional assistance, some exclusive options
  5. Allied: Full access to faction resources, regular support, unique quests and rewards
  6. Leadership: Ability to direct faction activities, represent in negotiations, maximum benefits

Building Reputation

Players can improve their standing with factions through:

  • Completing faction-specific quests
  • Supporting faction interests in council meetings
  • Developing projects that align with faction values
  • Building personal relationships with faction members
  • Trading resources valued by the faction
  • Defending faction interests against opponents

Note: Improving reputation with one faction may damage relationships with opposing factions. Players must make strategic choices about which alliances to prioritize.

Faction-Specific Gameplay Elements

Green Spark Collective

  • Unique Resources: Heirloom seeds, composting systems, community labor
  • Special Abilities: Community garden expansion, skill-sharing workshops, grassroots organizing
  • Headquarters Features: Seed library, community kitchen, meeting spaces, demonstration gardens
  • Endgame Content: Creating a regional sustainability network, hosting a sustainability conference

MegaGrow Industries

  • Unique Resources: Advanced technology, corporate funding, GMO crops
  • Special Abilities: Rapid development, political influence, technological solutions
  • Headquarters Features: Research labs, executive offices, demonstration farm, marketing center
  • Endgame Content: Reforming corporate practices, developing truly sustainable corporate model

Greenville City Council

  • Unique Resources: Public funding, regulatory authority, public spaces
  • Special Abilities: Policy changes, infrastructure development, public events
  • Headquarters Features: Council chambers, public records, grant office, planning department
  • Endgame Content: Comprehensive city planning, regional policy leadership
