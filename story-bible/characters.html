Characters

This section details the player character options, major NPCs, character archetypes, and the concept of community as character in HomeSalad.

Player Character

In HomeSalad, players create their own character who arrives in Greenville with skills in sustainability and community building. The player character is customizable but has a defined role in the narrative.

Character Creation

Players can customize their character with the following options:

Customization Category Options Gameplay Impact
Appearance Gender, body type, skin tone, hair style/color, facial features, clothing Primarily cosmetic, affects how NPCs initially address the character
Background Urban Planner, Environmental Scientist, Community Organizer, Permaculture Designer, Traditional Farmer Provides starting skill bonuses and unique dialogue options
Specialization Gardening, Building, Social, Technology, Crafting Determines initial skill levels and starting equipment
Motivation Environmental Restoration, Community Building, Personal Growth, Economic Opportunity, Family Connection Influences relationship development with certain NPCs and faction reputation gains

Character Role

Regardless of customization choices, the player character has a defined role in the narrative:

  • A newcomer to Greenville with expertise in sustainable practices
  • Someone seeking to put their knowledge into practice in a real community
  • A potential bridge between different factions and community members
  • A catalyst for change who can inspire others to action

The player's choices throughout the game will determine how this role evolves, from community member to potential leader of the Green Spark Collective or even a reformer within MegaGrow Industries.

Major NPCs

HomeSalad features a cast of fully developed NPCs who drive the narrative and provide gameplay opportunities. Each major NPC has:

  • A personal history tied to Greenville
  • A unique perspective on the community's challenges
  • Special skills or resources they can share
  • A personal quest line that develops their character
  • A relationship system with the player that evolves over time

Elena Chen

Role: Founder of the Green Spark Collective

Age: 35

Location: The Greenhouse (Downtown Commons)

Faction: Green Spark Collective

A former environmental scientist who returned to her hometown after witnessing ecological destruction in her research work. Elena founded the Green Spark Collective to create practical solutions to environmental problems. She's passionate, knowledgeable, and sometimes struggles with balancing idealism and pragmatism.

Elena serves as an early mentor to the player, introducing them to the community and the basics of sustainable practices. Her personal quest line involves reconciling with her father, who works for MegaGrow, and finding ways to make the Collective more inclusive and effective.

Gameplay Contributions:

  • Teaches advanced gardening techniques
  • Provides access to rare seeds and plants
  • Connects player with environmental networks
  • Offers strategic guidance for community organizing

Victor Reynolds

Role: CEO of MegaGrow Industries

Age: 52

Location: Corporate Tower (visits Downtown office)

Faction: MegaGrow Industries

A charismatic business leader who believes that corporate efficiency and technology are the solutions to environmental and economic challenges. Victor grew up in a farming community that struggled with traditional methods, shaping his belief in industrial agriculture. He's not villainous but represents a fundamentally different approach to sustainability.

Victor initially appears as an antagonist but can become a complex ally if the player chooses to work with MegaGrow to reform it from within. His personal quest line explores his growing awareness of the limitations of purely technological solutions and his relationship with his daughter, who secretly supports the Green Spark Collective.

Gameplay Contributions:

  • Provides access to advanced agricultural technology
  • Offers corporate funding for large projects
  • Controls access to significant land resources
  • Influences city council decisions

Maria Gonzalez

Role: Community Elder and Master Gardener

Age: 78

Location: Riverside Heights

Faction: Riverside Elders Association

A respected elder who has maintained traditional gardening practices throughout her life. Maria's family has lived in Greenville for generations, and she carries knowledge of heirloom crops, seasonal rhythms, and cultural traditions. She's warm but can be skeptical of new approaches that dismiss traditional wisdom.

Maria serves as a connection to Greenville's past and a source of deep agricultural knowledge. Her personal quest line involves documenting her knowledge for future generations and reconciling traditional practices with new sustainable techniques.

Gameplay Contributions:

  • Provides heirloom seeds with unique properties
  • Teaches companion planting and natural pest control
  • Shares stories that reveal Greenville's history
  • Connects player with elder community members

Jackson Reed

Role: Former Factory Worker, Maker

Age: 42

Location: The Workshop (Rust Belt District)

Faction: Rust Belt Reclamation Crew

A skilled craftsman who lost his manufacturing job when the factories closed. Jackson has adapted by learning to repurpose industrial materials into useful tools and structures. He's practical, resourceful, and sometimes bitter about the economic changes that have affected his community.

Jackson represents the working-class perspective in Greenville and the potential for industrial skills to be repurposed for sustainability. His personal quest line involves developing a worker-owned cooperative to create green infrastructure and finding new purpose in the changing economy.

Gameplay Contributions:

  • Teaches building and crafting techniques
  • Provides access to salvaged materials
  • Helps construct larger community projects
  • Connects player with former factory workers

Mayor Robert Thompson

Role: Mayor of Greenville

Age: 61

Location: City Hall (Downtown Commons)

Faction: Greenville City Council

A career politician trying to balance competing interests in a struggling town. Mayor Thompson genuinely wants what's best for Greenville but is caught between MegaGrow's promises of economic revival and grassroots demands for community control. He's diplomatic, cautious, and sometimes indecisive.

The Mayor serves as a gatekeeper to official resources and approvals. His personal quest line involves finding his political courage and deciding what legacy he wants to leave for Greenville.

Gameplay Contributions:

  • Controls access to public land and resources
  • Influences council votes on player projects
  • Provides official recognition for initiatives
  • Connects player with regional political networks

Character Archetypes

In addition to the major NPCs, HomeSalad features several character archetypes that appear throughout the game. These characters share certain traits but have individual personalities and stories:

Archetype Description Gameplay Role Examples
Community Gardeners Residents who participate in garden projects with varying levels of experience and commitment Provide labor for garden projects, share local knowledge, create social connections Sam the enthusiastic beginner, Delia the plant expert, Marcus the skeptic-turned-supporter
Local Business Owners Shopkeepers and service providers trying to survive in Greenville's changing economy Sell supplies, buy produce, offer specialized services, provide economic perspective Mei at the General Store, Carlos at the Café, Leila at the Farmers Market
Corporate Representatives MegaGrow employees with different levels of commitment to corporate values Present corporate viewpoint, offer technological solutions, create obstacles or opportunities Diana the true believer, James the conflicted scientist, Raj the pragmatic manager
Environmental Activists Dedicated environmentalists with different approaches to advocacy and action Provide environmental information, assist with projects, organize protests or initiatives Zoe the radical, Thomas the policy wonk, Aisha the community educator
Youth of Greenville Young residents caught between leaving for opportunities elsewhere and building a future in their hometown Bring energy to projects, connect with social media, represent future of community Jayden the tech-savvy teen, Lily the aspiring farmer, Miguel the community organizer

Community as Character

In HomeSalad, the community of Greenville itself functions as a character that evolves throughout the game:

Community States

The community progresses through several states based on player actions:

  1. Fragmented: Initial state with low trust, limited cooperation, and environmental degradation
  2. Awakening: Beginning to collaborate on small projects, growing awareness of possibilities
  3. Organizing: Forming more structured initiatives, developing shared vision and resources
  4. Transforming: Implementing major projects, changing physical and social landscape
  5. Thriving: Sustainable systems in place, resilient social networks, environmental restoration

Community Metrics

The game tracks several metrics that reflect the state of the community:

  • Environmental Health: Measures biodiversity, pollution levels, and ecosystem function
  • Social Cohesion: Tracks cooperation, trust, and collective efficacy
  • Economic Resilience: Measures local economic activity, resource circulation, and self-sufficiency
  • Knowledge Base: Tracks preservation and development of sustainable practices
  • Physical Infrastructure: Measures development of community spaces and sustainable systems

Community Voice

The community's collective perspective is expressed through:

  • Town hall meetings where diverse viewpoints are presented
  • Community bulletin boards with notices and comments
  • Local newspaper articles that summarize community developments
  • Overheard conversations that change to reflect community sentiment
  • Visual transformations that show the physical impact of community change

Design Note: The concept of "community as character" is central to HomeSalad's narrative design. While individual NPCs have their own stories and development, the collective transformation of Greenville provides the game's emotional core and measures the player's true progress.

Character Relationships

HomeSalad features a relationship system that tracks the player's connections with individual NPCs:

Relationship Dimensions

  • Trust: How much the NPC believes in the player's reliability and intentions
  • Respect: How much the NPC values the player's skills and accomplishments
  • Affinity: How much the NPC personally likes and connects with the player

Relationship Development

Relationships develop through:

  • Dialogue choices that reveal character values and perspectives
  • Completing personal quests for NPCs
  • Including NPCs in community projects
  • Regular interactions and activities
  • Gift-giving based on NPC preferences

Relationship Benefits

Strong relationships provide:

  • Access to unique skills and knowledge
  • Special items and resources
  • Additional quest opportunities
  • Support during community events and challenges
  • Deeper narrative content and character development
