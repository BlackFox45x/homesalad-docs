Environmental Systems

This section details the seasons, weather, ecosystems, and environmental challenges that shape the world of HomeSalad and drive key gameplay mechanics.

Seasonal Cycle

HomeSalad features a dynamic seasonal cycle that affects gameplay, narrative pacing, and environmental conditions. The game year is divided into four distinct seasons, each lasting approximately 28 in-game days.

Spring (Days 1-28)

Environmental Characteristics: Increasing daylight, warming temperatures, frequent rain showers, blooming wildflowers, returning wildlife, and flowing streams.

Gameplay Impact: Optimal time for planting most crops, increased water availability, special spring-only plants available.

Summer (Days 29-56)

Environmental Characteristics: Long daylight hours, warm to hot temperatures, occasional thunderstorms, lush vegetation, active wildlife.

Gameplay Impact: Fastest plant growth rates, early harvests begin, water management becomes critical.

Fall (Days 57-84)

Environmental Characteristics: Decreasing daylight, cooling temperatures, less frequent rain, changing leaf colors, migrating wildlife.

Gameplay Impact: Main harvest season, optimal time for seed collection, food preservation activities increase.

Winter (Days 85-112)

Environmental Characteristics: Short daylight hours, cold temperatures, occasional snow, dormant vegetation, reduced wildlife activity.

Gameplay Impact: Outdoor growing limited, indoor growing essential, energy management critical, planning for next year.

Weather System

HomeSalad features a dynamic weather system that affects gameplay, visual aesthetics, and environmental conditions. Weather patterns are influenced by season but include random variations that create unique challenges and opportunities.

Weather Types

  • Clear Sunny: Increased solar energy production, faster plant growth, higher water needs
  • Partly Cloudy: Balanced conditions for most activities
  • Overcast: Reduced solar energy production, slower plant growth
  • Light Rain: Natural irrigation, filled water collection systems
  • Heavy Rain: Potential flooding, erosion risks, reduced outdoor work
  • Thunderstorm: Potential damage to structures, power fluctuations
  • Snow: Insulation for some plants, reduced mobility, increased heating needs
  • Drought: Water scarcity, plant stress, increased fire risk

Ecosystems

Greenville contains several interconnected ecosystems that players can restore, enhance, and integrate into their sustainability projects.

Riparian Corridor

The ecosystem along the Green River and its tributaries, including riverbanks, floodplain, and adjacent wetlands.

Environmental Services: Water filtration, flood control, wildlife habitat, temperature regulation

Restoration Activities: Planting native vegetation, erosion control, creating buffer zones

Oak-Hickory Forest

Remnant forest patches on the hills surrounding Greenville, dominated by oak and hickory trees.

Environmental Services: Carbon sequestration, soil formation, wildlife habitat, water cycle regulation

Restoration Activities: Reforestation, creating corridors, establishing understory plants

Prairie and Meadow

Open grassland areas with diverse flowering plants, now rare due to agricultural conversion.

Environmental Services: Pollinator habitat, soil building, water infiltration, pest control

Restoration Activities: Seeding native mixes, establishing corridors, converting lawns

Wetland

Areas where water covers the soil or is present at or near the surface for varying periods.

Environmental Services: Water filtration, flood control, groundwater recharge, specialized habitat

Restoration Activities: Restoring hydrology, creating constructed wetlands, planting native vegetation

Environmental Challenges

Greenville faces several environmental challenges that drive the narrative and provide gameplay opportunities.

Water Pollution

Sources: Industrial runoff, agricultural chemicals, stormwater runoff, inadequate wastewater treatment

Solution Opportunities: Constructed wetlands, riparian buffers, bioremediation, green infrastructure

Soil Degradation

Sources: Industrial contamination, conventional agriculture, erosion, compaction, loss of organic matter

Solution Opportunities: Soil testing, cover cropping, composting, mycoremediation, erosion control

Biodiversity Loss

Sources: Habitat destruction, invasive species, pollution, climate change, monoculture

Solution Opportunities: Native plant restoration, wildlife corridors, invasive removal, habitat diversity

Environmental Transformation

A core narrative and gameplay element in HomeSalad is the visible transformation of Greenville's environment through player actions.

Transformation Dimensions

  • Visual: Landscape changes from degraded to restored with more diverse vegetation and wildlife
  • Functional: Ecosystems provide services like water filtration, pollination, and pest control
  • Social: Community members develop new relationships with the environment
  • Economic: New opportunities emerge based on sustainable resource use

Transformation Metrics

  • Biodiversity Index: Variety and abundance of species
  • Water Quality Rating: Cleanliness and health of water bodies
  • Soil Health Score: Organic matter, microbial activity, contamination levels
  • Carbon Sequestration: Carbon stored in plants and soil
  • Resource Circularity: How effectively resources are reused
